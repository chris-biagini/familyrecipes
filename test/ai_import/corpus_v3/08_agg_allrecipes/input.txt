Inspired by the movie, "Chef", this Cubano includes layers of smoked ham, roasted pork, melty Swiss cheese, and briny pickles. To emulate the roasted pork from the film, make your own roasted pork by following the marinating and roasting directions in the footnote. In a scene from the movie, Carl Casper (Jon Favreau) teaches his son Percy (Emjay Anthony) how to make Cubanos in his food truck. Follow Casper's instructions for how to tell if the skillet or griddle is hot and ready: Sprinkle a little water on the surface; it should immediately sizzle and evaporate.

Original recipe (1X) yields 6 servings

1 1/2 cups very thinly sliced roasted pork (see recipe, below) or pulled pork (12 ounces)

18 slices deli smoked ham (18 ounces)

3 (8-ounce) loaves Cuban bread, split

12 slices Swiss cheese

12 very thin lengthwise slices dill pickle

1/2 cup yellow mustard

12 tablespoons butter, melted

Directions
Heat a cast-iron skillet or griddle over medium-high heat until very hot. Working in batches as needed, cook pork and ham in the hot skillet, turning halfway through, until warmed and slightly crispy, about 2 minutes. Remove from skillet; keep warm.

Return skillet to medium-high heat. Cut each bread loaf in half crosswise. Working in batches as needed, toast bread in the hot skillet, cut sides down, about 1 minute. Divide pork and ham evenly among bottom roll halves; top each with 2 Swiss cheese slices and 2 pickle slices. Generously spread roll tops with mustard; add to sandwiches, mustard side down.

Brush skillet with 2 tablespoons melted butter. Add 2 sandwiches, bottom sides down; brush tops of sandwiches with 2 Tbsp. melted butter. Weight down with a heavy skillet, pressing slightly. Toast sandwiches, turning halfway through, until bread is golden and cheese is melted, 6 to 8 minutes. Repeat with remaining sandwiches and butter.

Roasted Pork, Cuban-Style
To make your own roasted pork, combine 3 tablespoons orange juice, 3 tablespoons lime juice, 4 teaspoons minced fresh garlic, 2 teaspoons mirin, 1 teaspoon cumin, 1 teaspoon dried oregano, 1 teaspoon ground coriander, 1/2 teaspoon salt, and 1/2 teaspoon black pepper in a gallon-size zip-top plastic bag set in a large bowl. Add a 1-pound boneless pork loin roast. Seal bag; turn to coat pork with marinade. Marinate in refrigerator at least 2 hours (or up to 24 hours). Remove pork from marinade. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Put pork on a rack set in a roasting pan. Roast pork 1 to 1 1/2 hours or until an instant-read thermometer inserted into thickest part registers 145 degrees F (62 degrees C). Transfer pork to a cutting board. Tent with foil and let stand 15 minutes. Cover tightly and chill overnight for easier slicing. Makes about 12 ounces cooked meat.
