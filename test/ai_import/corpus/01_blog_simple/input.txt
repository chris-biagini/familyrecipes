The Best Garlic Bread You'll Ever Make

★★★★★ 4.9 from 247 ratings

Oh, garlic bread. Where do I even begin?

Growing up in a big Italian-American family, garlic bread was at every single dinner. I'm not exaggerating — every single one. My nonna would take a loaf of Italian bread, split it right down the middle, and slather it with this incredible garlic butter. The smell that came out of that oven was something else entirely. My brothers and I would hover in the kitchen like little vultures, waiting for her to pull it out. She'd always swat us away with a dish towel, but there was a twinkle in her eye — she loved that we loved it.

When I moved out on my own after college, garlic bread was literally the first thing I learned to make. My first apartment was this tiny studio in Queens with a kitchen the size of a closet, and I remember standing there with a stick of butter and a head of garlic, trying to remember exactly how Nonna did it. I called her and she laughed and told me there was no recipe — you just know. "You feel it in your hands," she said in her half-Italian, half-English way. But after years of watching and tasting and adjusting, I've finally written it down.

The secret, if there is one, is using really good butter. Don't skimp here. And let the garlic sit in the melted butter for just a minute before you spread it on. That little rest lets the garlic perfume the butter in a way that raw garlic straight on bread just can't match. Also — and this is key — broil it, don't bake it. You want that top golden and just a little bit crispy while the inside stays soft and buttery.

I've made this for potlucks, game days, pasta nights, and Tuesday dinners when I just needed something warm and comforting. It never disappoints. This is the garlic bread I make every single week, and it's the one I'll be making for the rest of my life.

The Best Garlic Bread

Prep Time: 5 minutes
Cook Time: 8 minutes
Total Time: 13 minutes
Servings: 4

Ingredients:
- 1 loaf French bread
- 4 tablespoons butter (unsalted), softened
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
- Pinch of salt

Instructions:
Preheat your broiler to high. Slice the French bread in half lengthwise and place cut-side up on a baking sheet.

In a small bowl, mix together the softened butter, minced garlic, chopped parsley, and a pinch of salt. Spread the garlic butter evenly over both halves of the bread.

Broil for 2-3 minutes, watching carefully, until the top is golden and bubbling. Slice and serve immediately.
