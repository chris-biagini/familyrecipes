SUNDAY SUPPERS


   Pasta with Meat Sauce

A rich, slow-simmered meat sauce over tender spaghetti. This is
the kind of Sunday supper that fills the whole house with the
most incredible smell.

Serves 4 to 6

For the Sauce:
   l tablespoon olive oil
   1 lb ground beef
   l/2   cup diced onion
   3 cloves garlic, minced
   1 can (28 oz) crushed tomatoes
   2 tablespoons tomato paste
   l teaspoon dried oregano
   l/2 teaspoon dried basil
   3⁄4 teaspoon salt
   l/4 teaspoon black pepper
   1 teaspoon sugar

For the Pasta:
   1 lb spaghetti
   Salt for the cooking water
   Parmesan cheese, for serving
   Fresh basil-- for garnish

Heat the olive oil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Add
the ground beef and cook, breaking it up with a wooden spoon, until
browned and no longer pink, about 6-8 minutes. Drain any excess fat.

Add the diced onion and cook for 3-4 minutes until softened. Add the
garlic and cook for 3O seconds until fragrant.

Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, basil, salt,
pepper, and sugar. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to low. Cover
and let the sauce cook for at least 3O minutes, stirring occasionally.
The longer it simmers the better it gets--up to 2 hours if you have
the time.

Bring a large pot of generously salted water to a rolling boil. Cook
the spaghetti according to package directions until al dente. Drain,
reserving l/2 cup of the pasta water.

Toss the hot pasta with the meat sauce, adding a splash of pasta water
to loosen the sauce if needed. Serve in deep bowls topped with grated
Parmesan and a few leaves of fresh basil.
