       Drop Biscuits

A simple biscuit that comes together in minutes. No rolling or cutting needed —
just drop them onto the sheet and bake.

Makes about 12 biscuits

   2 cups all-purpose flour
   1 tablespoon baking powder
   1 teaspoon saltand 1/2 teaspoon sugar
   1/3 cup cold butter, cut into small pieces
   3/4 cup whole milk
   1 tablespoon honey

Preheat your oven to 425 degrees F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt,
and sugar. Add the cold butter pieces and use a pastry cutter or
your fingers to work the butter into the flour until the mixture
resembles coarse crumbs with some pea-sized pieces remaining.
Pour in the milk and honey and stir with a fork just until the
dough comes together. Do not overmix — a few lumps are fine.

Using a large spoon, drop roughly 1/4 cup mounds of dough onto
the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart.
Bake for 12-14 minutes until the tops are golden brown. Serve
warm with butter and jam.
