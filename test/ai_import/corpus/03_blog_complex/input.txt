The Absolute Best Cinnamon Rolls — Soft, Gooey, and Perfect for Christmas Morning

★★★★★ 4.9 from 412 ratings

Jump to Recipe | Print Recipe | Save to Pinterest

Every family has that one recipe that means Christmas. For us, it's cinnamon rolls.

I can still picture my mom in her flannel robe at 5:30 in the morning, pulling a pan of golden, pillowy cinnamon rolls out of the oven while the rest of us were still rubbing sleep from our eyes. The whole house smelled like cinnamon and butter and yeast and warmth. We'd crowd around the kitchen island — my dad, my sister, me — and my mom would drizzle the cream cheese glaze over the top while the rolls were still steaming. We each had our favorite — I always wanted a corner piece because you get extra glaze pooling around the edges.

When my mom passed in 2019, I was terrified I'd lose the recipe. She never wrote things down. But my sister and I spent a whole weekend that January in my kitchen, testing and tasting and calling our Aunt Linda, who had watched Mom make them a hundred times. It took four batches, but we got it. These are Mom's cinnamon rolls, or as close as two determined daughters could get.

I've been making them every Christmas morning since, and now my own kids hover around the kitchen island just like I used to. The tradition lives on.

I know cinnamon rolls seem intimidating, but I promise you can do this. The dough is forgiving, the filling is dead simple, and the glaze is literally four ingredients. Let's make some memories.

The Best Cinnamon Rolls

Prep Time: 30 minutes
Rise Time: 2 hours
Cook Time: 25 minutes
Total Time: 2 hours 55 minutes
Yield: 12 rolls

FOR THE DOUGH:
- ¾ cup whole milk, warmed to 110°F
- 2¼ teaspoons active dry yeast (1 packet)
- ¼ cup sugar
- 1 large egg plus 1 egg yolk, at room temperature
- ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted
- 3 cups all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
- ¾ teaspoon salt

FOR THE FILLING:
- ⅔ cup brown sugar, packed
- 1½ tablespoons cinnamon
- ¼ cup unsalted butter, softened

TIP: For the gooiest rolls, don't skip the full rise time. I know it's tempting to rush, but patience makes all the difference here.

FOR THE CREAM CHEESE GLAZE:
- 4 ounces cream cheese, softened
- 1 cup powdered sugar
- ½ teaspoon vanilla extract
- 2-3 tablespoons whole milk

Instructions:

1. In a large bowl, combine the warmed milk, yeast, and a pinch of the sugar. Let sit for 5-7 minutes until foamy. This tells you the yeast is alive and ready to work.

2. Add the remaining sugar, egg, egg yolk, and melted butter to the yeast mixture. Stir to combine. Add the flour and salt and stir until a shaggy dough forms.

3. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. The dough should spring back when poked with a finger.

TIP: If the dough feels too sticky, add flour a tablespoon at a time. Too much flour makes dense rolls — err on the side of slightly sticky.

4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with plastic wrap or a damp towel, and let rise in a warm spot for 1 to 1½ hours until doubled in size.

5. While the dough rises, mix together the brown sugar and cinnamon for the filling.

6. Punch down the dough and roll out on a floured surface into a large rectangle, about 16 by 12 inches. Spread the softened butter evenly over the surface, leaving a ½-inch border along one long edge. Sprinkle the cinnamon sugar mixture over the butter.

7. Starting from the long edge opposite the border, roll the dough up tightly into a log. Pinch the seam to seal. Using a sharp knife or unflavored dental floss, cut the log into 12 equal pieces.

TIP: Dental floss gives you the cleanest cuts without squishing the rolls. Slide it under the log, cross the ends over the top, and pull.

8. Place the rolls cut-side up in a greased 9x13-inch baking dish. Cover and let rise for 30-45 minutes until the rolls are puffy and touching.

9. Preheat your oven to 375°F. Bake for 22-25 minutes until the rolls are golden brown on top. Don't overbake — they'll firm up as they cool.

10. While the rolls bake, make the glaze. Beat the cream cheese until smooth. Add the powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and 2 tablespoons of milk. Beat until creamy, adding more milk a little at a time until you reach your desired consistency.

11. Drizzle the glaze over the warm rolls. Serve immediately.

TIP: You can make these the night before! After cutting and placing the rolls in the pan (step 8), cover tightly and refrigerate overnight. In the morning, pull them out, let them come to room temperature for about 30 minutes, then bake as directed.
