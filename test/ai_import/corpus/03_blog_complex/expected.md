# Cinnamon Rolls

Soft, gooey, and perfect for Christmas morning.

Makes: 12 rolls
Category: Baking
Tags: holiday

## Make the dough.

- Milk (whole), 3/4 cup: Warmed to 110°F.
- Yeast (active dry), 2 1/4 tsp
- Sugar (white), 1/4 cup
- Egg, 1
- Egg yolk, 1
- Butter (unsalted), 1/4 cup: Melted.
- Flour (all-purpose), 3 cups
- Salt, 3/4 tsp

In a large bowl, combine the warmed milk, yeast, and a pinch of the sugar. Let sit for 5-7 minutes until foamy. This tells you the yeast is alive and ready to work.

Add the remaining sugar, egg, egg yolk, and melted butter to the yeast mixture. Stir to combine. Add the flour and salt and stir until a shaggy dough forms.

Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. The dough should spring back when poked with a finger.

Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with plastic wrap or a damp towel, and let rise in a warm spot for 1 to 1 1/2 hours until doubled in size.

## Fill and shape the rolls.

- Sugar (brown), 2/3 cup
- Cinnamon, 1 1/2 tbsp
- Butter (unsalted), 1/4 cup: Softened.

While the dough rises, mix together the brown sugar and cinnamon for the filling.

Punch down the dough and roll out on a floured surface into a large rectangle, about 16 by 12 inches. Spread the softened butter evenly over the surface, leaving a 1/2-inch border along one long edge. Sprinkle the cinnamon sugar mixture over the butter.

Starting from the long edge opposite the border, roll the dough up tightly into a log. Pinch the seam to seal. Using a sharp knife or unflavored dental floss, cut the log into 12 equal pieces.

Place the rolls cut-side up in a greased 9x13-inch baking dish. Cover and let rise for 30-45 minutes until the rolls are puffy and touching.

## Bake the rolls.

Preheat your oven to 375°F. Bake for 22-25 minutes until the rolls are golden brown on top. Don't overbake — they'll firm up as they cool.

## Make the glaze.

- Cream cheese, 4 oz: Softened.
- Sugar (powdered), 1 cup
- Vanilla extract, 1/2 tsp
- Milk (whole), 2-3 tbsp

While the rolls bake, make the glaze. Beat the cream cheese until smooth. Add the powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and 2 tablespoons of milk. Beat until creamy, adding more milk a little at a time until you reach your desired consistency.

Drizzle the glaze over the warm rolls. Serve immediately.

---

For the gooiest rolls, don't skip the full rise time. If the dough feels too sticky, add flour a tablespoon at a time — too much flour makes dense rolls. Dental floss gives the cleanest cuts without squishing the rolls. You can make these the night before: after placing the rolls in the pan, cover tightly and refrigerate overnight, then let them come to room temperature for about 30 minutes before baking.
