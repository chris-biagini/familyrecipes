# Spaghetti Bolognese

Quick weeknight bolognese with real depth.

Serves: 5
Category: Mains
Tags: weeknight, quick, italian

## Make the sauce.

- Olive oil, 1 1/2 tbsp
- Onion, 1: Finely chopped.
- Garlic, 2 cloves: Minced.
- Beef mince (ground beef), 500 g
- Dry red wine, 125 ml
- Beef bouillon cubes, 2: Crumbled.
- Tomatoes (crushed, canned), 800 g
- Tomato paste, 2 tbsp
- Sugar (white), 2 tsp
- Worcestershire sauce, 2 tsp
- Bay leaves (dried), 2
- Thyme (fresh), 2 sprigs
- Salt, 3/4 tsp
- Black pepper, 1/2 tsp

Heat oil in large pot over medium-high. Sauté onion and garlic about 3 minutes until softened and light golden. Crank heat to high, add beef, and cook breaking it up until browned. Add wine, scrape bottom, and simmer 1 minute until alcohol smell is gone.

Add all remaining sauce ingredients. Stir, bring to simmer, reduce to medium, and cook uncovered 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add a splash of water if sauce gets too thick.

## Cook pasta and serve.

- Spaghetti (dried), 400 g
- Parmesan: Optional.
- Parsley: Optional.

Cook spaghetti in well-salted boiling water per packet minus 1 minute. Reserve a mug of pasta water before draining. Add drained pasta to sauce with about 125 ml pasta water over medium heat; toss 1 1/2-2 minutes until pasta turns red and sauce clings. Taste and adjust salt. Serve garnished with parmesan and parsley.

---

Substitute half pork mince for a richer sauce. Red wine can be replaced with water or beef stock. Crushed tomatoes can be replaced with tomato passata. Use 1/2 tsp dried thyme or oregano in place of fresh thyme sprigs. Sugar is only needed if the sauce tastes sharp — add to taste.

**Slow simmer option:** After adding sauce ingredients, add 3/4 cup water, cover, and simmer on very low for 2-2 1/2 hours, stirring every 30 minutes. Uncover and simmer 20 minutes to thicken.
