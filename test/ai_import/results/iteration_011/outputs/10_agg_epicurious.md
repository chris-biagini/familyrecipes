# Good Eats Beef Stew

Serves: 4
Category: Mains

## Make paste; sear and slow-cook ribs.

- Tomato paste, 1/4 cup
- Apple cider vinegar, 1/4 cup
- Worcestershire sauce, 1 1/2 tsp
- Paprika, 1 tbsp
- Dried herbs (thyme, oregano, and/or rosemary), 1 1/2 tsp
- Short ribs (English-cut), 3 lbs
- Salt (kosher), 1 tbsp

Whisk together tomato paste, vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, paprika, and dried herbs. Set aside.

Season ribs with salt. Sear over medium-high heat until browned on all sides. Toss in paste to coat. Wrap tightly in heavy-duty foil, place in a metal pan, and put into a cold oven on the middle rack. Set to 250°F and cook 4 hours.

## Drain and degrease braising liquid.

Remove from oven; carefully poke a hole in the foil and drain liquid into a heatproof container. Refrigerate about 1 hour until fat separates, then transfer to freezer until fat cap solidifies, about 1 more hour. Leave ribs at room temperature while liquid cools.

## Cook vegetables; finish and serve.

- Onion, 1 large: Thinly sliced.
- Salt (kosher), 1 tsp
- Red potatoes, 1 lb: Unpeeled, sliced.
- Black pepper, a pinch
- Parsley, 1 tbsp: Chopped.

Skim fat cap from frozen liquid. Reserve 1 tbsp of fat; save the rest for another use. Melt reserved fat in a large saucier over medium heat. Add onion and salt, stir into rings, and cook 2-3 minutes. Add potatoes and pepper, then add braising liquid. Cover tightly, reduce to low, and cook until potatoes are fork-tender, about 30 minutes.

Cut meat from bone, discarding connective tissue. Nestle meat atop vegetables, cover, and cook 10 more minutes. Serve sprinkled with parsley.
