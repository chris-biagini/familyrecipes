# Beef Wellington

Serves: 6
Category: Mains
Tags: holiday

## Sear beef; make duxelles.

- Beef tenderloin (center-cut), 2 lbs
- Salt
- Black pepper
- Vegetable oil, 2 tbsp + 1/2 tsp
- Horseradish (prepared), 2 tbsp
- Dijon mustard, 1 tbsp
- Mushrooms, 1 lb: Cleaned, trimmed, roughly chopped.
- Butter (unsalted), 4 tbsp
- Shallots, 2: Finely diced.
- Thyme (fresh), 2 tsp: Finely minced.
- Cognac, 1/2 cup
- Heavy cream, 1/2 cup
- Soy sauce, 2 tsp
- Foie gras (fresh), 4 oz

Tie tenderloin at 1-inch intervals with butcher's twine; season liberally with salt and pepper. Heat 2 tbsp oil in cast iron over high heat until smoking. Sear tenderloin without moving until well browned on one side, about 2 minutes; rotate and continue until browned all over, about 10 minutes total. Transfer to a plate.

Combine horseradish, mustard, and 1 tsp black pepper. When cool enough to handle, remove twine, rub tenderloin all over with mustard mixture, and refrigerate uncovered.

Pulse mushrooms in food processor in two batches until very finely chopped, about 10 short pulses per batch. Heat butter in a large skillet over medium-high. Add mushrooms; cook, stirring occasionally, until liquid evaporates and mushrooms sizzle, about 8 minutes. Continue until browned, about 4 minutes more. Add shallots and thyme; cook, stirring, until softened, about 2 minutes. Add Cognac; scrape up browned bits and cook until nearly dry, about 4 minutes. Add cream and soy sauce; cook, stirring frequently, until thick and mixture collects in one mass when pan is shaken. Season with salt and pepper. Transfer to a bowl and refrigerate.

Season foie gras with salt and pepper. Heat remaining 1/2 tsp oil in a small skillet over high heat until smoking. Sear foie gras without moving, about 30 seconds per side, until well browned. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate. Pour rendered fat into mushroom mixture and stir to combine. Halve each piece of foie gras horizontally. Refrigerate all components at least 30 minutes.

## Assemble and wrap.

- Phyllo dough, 1 sheet
- Prosciutto, 1/4 lb: Sliced paper-thin.
- Flour
- Puff pastry, 14 oz: Thawed.
- Egg, 1: Beaten.

Lay a double layer of plastic wrap about 2 feet long on a cutting board. Lay phyllo on top. Shingle prosciutto in an even, overlapping layer, leaving a 2-inch border along top and bottom. Spread mushroom mixture evenly over prosciutto.

Place tenderloin along the bottom edge. Lay foie gras slices evenly on top. Using the plastic wrap to help tighten, roll tenderloin up in the mushroom, ham, and phyllo. Re-wrap tightly in fresh plastic wrap, twisting ends to seal. Chill at least 30 minutes.

Dust surface lightly with flour. Roll puff pastry into a rectangle at least 4 inches wider than the roll on its shorter side. Unwrap beef roll and place foie gras-side up along the bottom edge of the pastry. Brush a 6-inch strip of pastry just above the roll with beaten egg. Roll beef in pastry until fully wrapped, foie gras-side ending up on top with seam on the bottom. Trim excess. Fold in the ends: fold side flaps toward center, fold top flaps down, trim bottom flaps; flip seam-side up, fold end flaps up to seal, then flip right-side up. Chill at least 30 minutes.

## Bake and serve.

- Coarse sea salt
- Chives, 1 bunch: Finely minced.

Preheat oven to 425°F, rack to center. Transfer Wellington to a foil-lined baking sheet. Brush all over with remaining beaten egg, score a decorative pattern in the pastry, and sprinkle liberally with coarse salt. Bake until pastry is golden brown and center registers 110°F for rare or 120°F for medium-rare, 35-45 minutes. Rest 10 minutes.

Loosen from foil with a thin spatula and transfer to a carving board. Slice off ends, carve, sprinkle cut surfaces with chives and more coarse salt, and serve.

---

Button, cremini, shiitake, portobello, or a mix work for the mushrooms. Substitute spicy brown or hot English mustard for the Dijon. Substitute other brandy or barrel-aged spirit (such as bourbon) for the Cognac. Foie gras pâté can substitute for fresh foie gras: skip the searing step and spread pâté directly over the tenderloin during assembly instead. Use high-quality all-butter puff pastry such as Dufour for best results. A center-cut beef tenderloin is also known as Châteaubriand. Coarse sea salt: Maldon or fleur de sel recommended. Special equipment: butcher's twine, instant-read thermometer.
