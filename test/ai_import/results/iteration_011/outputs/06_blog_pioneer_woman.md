# Beef-and-Bean Chili

Lots of beef, long simmer.

Serves: 6
Category: Mains

## Build and simmer.

- Ground beef, 4 lb
- Garlic, 6 cloves: Minced.
- Tomato sauce, 2 cans (8 oz each)
- Tomato paste, 1 can (6 oz)
- Chili powder, 1/4 cup
- Cumin, 2 tsp
- Oregano, 2 tsp
- Salt (kosher), 2 tsp
- Cayenne, 1/2 tsp

Brown beef with garlic in a large heavy pot. Drain most of the fat, leaving a little. Add tomato sauce, paste, chili powder, cumin, oregano, salt, and cayenne. Stir well, cover, reduce to low. Simmer 1 hour, stirring occasionally. If liquid runs low, add up to 1 1/2 cups water.

## Finish and serve.

- Beans (kidney and pinto), 4 cans (15 oz each): Drained and rinsed.
- Masa harina, 1/2 cup
- Cheddar: Shredded.
- Bacon: Crumbled.
- Jalapeño: Sliced.

Whisk masa harina with 1/2 cup water until smooth. Stir beans into chili, then stir in masa slurry. Simmer 10 more minutes to thicken. Taste and adjust seasonings. Top with cheddar, bacon, and jalapeño.

---

Cheddar, bacon, and jalapeño are toppings — garnish to taste.

Substitute cornmeal for masa harina.

Canned beans can be slimy straight from the can — rinse well.

Based on a recipe from Ree Drummond.
