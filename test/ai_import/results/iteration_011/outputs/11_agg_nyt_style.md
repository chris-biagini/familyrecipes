# No-Knead Bread

Patience over technique.

Makes: 1 loaf (1 1/2 lb)
Category: Bread

## Mix dough; ferment overnight.

- Flour (all-purpose), 3 1/3 cups (430 g)
- Yeast (instant), generous 1/4 tsp (1 g)
- Salt (kosher), 2 tsp (8 g)
- Water, 1 1/2 cups (345 g)

Combine flour, yeast, and salt in a large bowl. Stir in water until blended — dough will be shaggy and sticky. Cover and rest at least 12 hours, preferably 18, at about 70°F, until surface is dotted with bubbles.

## Shape and proof.

- Cornmeal

Turn dough onto a lightly floured surface; fold over once or twice. Cover loosely and rest 15 minutes.

Shape quickly into a ball using just enough flour to prevent sticking. Generously coat a cotton towel (not terry cloth) with flour, wheat bran, or cornmeal; place dough seam-side down and dust top with more. Cover with a second towel and let rise about 2 hours, until more than doubled and not springing back when poked.

## Bake.

At least 30 minutes before baking, heat oven to 450°F with a 6- to 8-qt heavy covered pot (cast iron, enamel, Pyrex, or ceramic) inside.

Remove hot pot. Turn dough in seam-side up — it may look like a mess, but that's fine. Shake once or twice if unevenly distributed. Cover and bake 30 minutes, then uncover and bake another 15-30 minutes until deeply browned. Cool on a rack.

---

Bread flour can be substituted for all-purpose. Wheat bran can be used in place of cornmeal.

Based on a recipe from Jim Lahey, as adapted by Mark Bittman.
