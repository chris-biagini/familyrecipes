# Caramelized Onion and Gruyère Biscuits

Big, cheesy, worth the onion time.

Makes: 10 biscuits
Category: Baking

## Caramelize onions.

- Butter (unsalted), 1 tbsp
- Olive oil, 1 tbsp
- Onion, 2 small: Halved and thinly sliced.

Melt butter with olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onions, reduce to low, cover and steam about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove lid; cook, stirring occasionally, until deep brown, 10-20 more minutes. Set aside to cool.

## Make dough; cut and bake.

- Flour (all-purpose), 2 3/4 cups (345 g)
- Sugar (white), 1 tbsp (15 g)
- Baking powder, 2 tsp
- Baking soda, 1 tsp
- Salt (kosher), 3/4 tsp
- Butter (unsalted), 8 tbsp: Cold, diced into 1/2-inch pieces.
- Gruyère, 4 oz (115 g): Cut into 1/2-inch cubes.
- Buttermilk, 1 cup
- Flaky sea salt
- Black pepper

Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment.

Combine flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Work in cold butter by hand or food processor until crumbly with pieces no larger than a pea. Transfer to a bowl if using a food processor. Stir in cheese. Combine buttermilk with cooled onions, then add to flour mixture; stir to combine, using hands if needed — a few floury spots are fine.

Turn onto a floured surface; roll to 1-inch thickness. Stamp out 3-inch circles with a floured cutter and space on prepared sheet. Re-roll scraps as needed. Sprinkle with flaky salt and pepper. Bake until deep golden-brown with bubbly cheese at edges, 20-23 minutes.

---

Best warm on day one; re-warm on day two. Substitute another Swiss-style cheese for the Gruyère. Coarse salt can substitute for kosher.

Based on a recipe from Alyce Shields at the late Pronto by Bar Bambino in SF, via Tasting Table.
