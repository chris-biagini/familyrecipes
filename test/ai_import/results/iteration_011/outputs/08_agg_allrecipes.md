# Cubano

From the 'Chef' food truck.

Serves: 6
Category: Mains

## Advance prep: roast pork.

- Orange juice, 3 tbsp
- Lime juice, 3 tbsp
- Garlic, 4 tsp: Minced.
- Mirin, 2 tsp
- Cumin, 1 tsp
- Oregano (dried), 1 tsp
- Coriander, 1 tsp
- Salt, 1/2 tsp
- Black pepper, 1/2 tsp
- Pork loin (boneless), 1 lb

Combine juices, garlic, mirin, and spices in a gallon zip-top bag. Add pork, seal, turn to coat. Marinate in fridge at least 2 hours or up to 24.

Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Remove pork from marinade, set on a rack in a roasting pan. Roast until an instant-read thermometer reads 145°F (62°C), 1-1 1/2 hours. Tent with foil, rest 15 minutes. Chill overnight for easier slicing. Yields about 12 oz cooked meat.

## Warm meat; toast bread.

- Roasted pork, 1 1/2 cups (12 oz): Very thinly sliced.
- Ham (smoked deli), 18 slices (18 oz)
- Cuban bread, 3 loaves (8 oz each): Split.

Heat cast-iron skillet or griddle over medium-high until very hot. Cook pork and ham in batches, turning once, until warmed and slightly crispy, about 2 minutes. Set aside and keep warm.

Toast bread cut-side down in batches, about 1 minute.

## Assemble and press.

- Swiss cheese, 12 slices
- Dill pickle, 12 slices: Very thin, lengthwise.
- Yellow mustard, 1/2 cup
- Butter, 12 tbsp: Melted.

Divide pork and ham among bottom bread halves. Top each with 2 cheese slices and 2 pickle slices. Spread mustard generously on top halves; close sandwiches mustard-side down.

Brush skillet with 2 tbsp butter. Add 2 sandwiches; brush tops with 2 tbsp butter. Weight down with a heavy skillet, pressing slightly. Toast, turning once, until golden and cheese is melted, 6-8 minutes. Repeat in batches with remaining butter.

---

Substitute pulled pork for the roasted pork. Based on a recipe from Jaelyn Luong (Allrecipes).
