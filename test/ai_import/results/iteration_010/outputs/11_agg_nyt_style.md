# No-Knead Bread

Patience, not technique.

Makes: 1 loaf
Category: Bread

## Mix and ferment.

- Flour (all-purpose), 3 1/3 cups (430 g)
- Yeast (instant), generous 1/4 tsp (1 g)
- Salt (kosher), 2 tsp (8 g)
- Water, 1 1/2 cups (345 g)

Combine flour, yeast, and salt in a large bowl. Add water and stir until blended — dough will be shaggy and sticky. Cover with plastic wrap and rest at warm room temperature (about 70°F) for at least 12 hours, preferably 18. Dough is ready when the surface is dotted with bubbles.

## Shape and proof.

- Cornmeal, as needed

Lightly flour a work surface, turn dough out, sprinkle with more flour, and fold over once or twice. Cover loosely with plastic wrap and rest 15 minutes.

Using just enough flour to prevent sticking, quickly shape dough into a ball. Generously coat a cotton (not terry cloth) towel with flour, bran, or cornmeal. Place dough seam side down on towel, dust the top, cover with a second towel, and proof about 2 hours — until more than doubled and dough doesn't spring back when poked.

## Bake.

About 30 minutes before dough is ready, heat oven to 450°F with a 6-8 qt heavy covered pot (cast iron, enamel, Pyrex, or ceramic) inside. Remove hot pot from oven and tip dough in seam side up — it will look messy. Shake once or twice if needed. Cover and bake 30 minutes, then uncover and bake 15-30 more minutes until deeply browned. Cool on a rack.

---

Bread flour may be substituted for all-purpose. Wheat bran may be substituted for cornmeal.

Based on a recipe from Jim Lahey.
