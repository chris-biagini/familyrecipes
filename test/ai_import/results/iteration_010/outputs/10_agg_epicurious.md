# Good Eats Beef Stew

Serves: 4
Category: Mains

## Make the braise.

- Tomato paste, 1/4 cup
- Apple cider vinegar, 1/4 cup
- Worcestershire sauce, 1 1/2 tsp
- Paprika, 1 tbsp
- Herbs (dried), 1 1/2 tsp
- Short ribs (English-cut), 3 lbs
- Salt (kosher), 1 tbsp

Whisk together tomato paste, vinegar, Worcestershire, paprika, and dried herbs.

Season ribs with salt. Heat griddle over medium-high until a water drop bounces. Sear until browned on all sides. Toss in paste mixture to coat. Transfer to a large piece of heavy-duty foil, seal tightly, and set in a metal pan. Place pan in a cold oven on the middle rack, then set to 250°F. Cook 4 hours.

## Defat the braising liquid.

Remove from oven. Carefully poke a hole in the foil over a heatproof container; drain liquid. Refrigerate about 1 hour until fat begins to separate, then transfer to the freezer until fat cap solidifies, about 1 more hour. Let ribs rest at room temperature while liquid cools.

## Cook the vegetables.

- Salt (kosher), 1 tsp
- Onion, 1: Thinly sliced.
- Potatoes (red), 1 lb: Sliced, unpeeled.
- Black pepper

Skim fat cap from cooled braising liquid; measure out 1 tbsp. Melt in a large saucier over medium heat. Add onion and salt; stir to separate into rings. Cook 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add potatoes and a pinch of black pepper, then pour in reserved braising liquid. Cover tightly and reduce to low. Cook until potatoes are fork-tender, about 30 minutes.

## Bone the meat; finish and serve.

- Parsley, 1 tbsp: Chopped.

While potatoes cook, cut meat from bone; remove and discard connective tissue.

When potatoes are tender, uncover and nestle meat atop vegetables. Cover and cook 10 more minutes. Serve sprinkled with parsley.

---

Dried herbs: use any combination of thyme, oregano, and rosemary. Remaining rendered fat can be reserved for another use.
