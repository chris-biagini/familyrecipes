# Beef Wellington

Serves: 6
Category: Mains
Tags: holiday

## Sear and coat the tenderloin.

- Beef tenderloin (center-cut), 2 lbs: Trimmed of silverskin and fat.
- Salt
- Black pepper
- Vegetable oil, 2 tbsp
- Horseradish (prepared), 2 tbsp
- Dijon mustard, 1 tbsp

Tie tenderloin at 1-inch intervals with butcher's twine; trim ends. Season liberally with salt and pepper.

Heat oil in a cast iron or stainless skillet over high heat until smoking. Sear, turning, until well browned on all sides, about 10 minutes total. Transfer to a plate.

Once cool enough to handle, cut off and discard twine. Combine horseradish, mustard, and 1 tsp black pepper; rub over all surfaces. Refrigerate, uncovered.

## Make duxelles; sear foie gras.

- Mushrooms, 1 lb: Roughly chopped.
- Butter (unsalted), 4 tbsp
- Shallots, 2: Finely diced.
- Thyme, 2 tsp: Minced.
- Cognac, 1/2 cup
- Heavy cream, 1/2 cup
- Soy sauce, 2 tsp
- Foie gras (fresh), 4 oz
- Vegetable oil, 1/2 tsp

Pulse mushrooms in a food processor in two batches until very finely chopped, about 10 short pulses per batch.

Melt butter in a 12-inch skillet over medium-high heat. Cook mushrooms, stirring occasionally, until all liquid has been released and they start to sizzle, about 8 minutes. Continue until browned and leaving fond on the pan bottom, about 4 minutes more. Add shallots and thyme; cook, stirring, until softened, about 2 minutes.

Add cognac and scrape up fond. Cook until nearly dry, about 4 minutes. Add cream and soy sauce; cook, stirring, until mixture is thick and collects into a single mass when the pan is shaken. Season with salt and pepper. Transfer to a bowl and refrigerate.

Season foie gras with salt and pepper. Heat oil in a small skillet over high heat until smoking. Sear foie gras without moving until well browned on first side, about 30 seconds. Flip and brown second side, about 30 seconds more. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate. Pour rendered fat into mushroom mixture and stir to combine. Halve each piece of foie gras horizontally. Refrigerate all components at least 30 minutes.

## Assemble; wrap in pastry.

- Phyllo dough, 1 sheet
- Prosciutto, 1/4 lb: Sliced paper thin.
- Flour
- Puff pastry, 14 oz: Thawed.
- Egg, 1: Beaten.

Lay a double layer of plastic wrap (about 2 × 1 feet) on a cutting board. Lay phyllo on top. Shingle prosciutto over phyllo in a thin, even, overlapping layer, leaving a 2-inch border along top and bottom edges. Spread mushroom mixture evenly over prosciutto.

Place tenderloin along the very bottom edge. Lay sliced foie gras evenly over top. Using the plastic wrap, roll tenderloin tightly in the mushroom/prosciutto/phyllo layer. Re-wrap in fresh plastic wrap, twisting ends very tight. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes.

Dust board lightly with flour. Roll puff pastry into a rectangle at least 4 inches wider than the roll on the shorter side. Unwrap beef roll and place along the bottom edge of pastry, foie gras side up. Brush 6 inches of pastry above the roll with beaten egg. Roll beef tightly in pastry until completely wrapped — foie gras side should face up again, seam on the bottom. Trim excess. Fold protruding side flaps toward center, fold top flaps down, trim bottom flaps. Flip roll over, fold up end flaps to seal completely, flip right-side up. Transfer to a plate and chill at least 30 minutes.

## Bake and serve.

- Sea salt (coarse)
- Chives, 1 bunch: Minced.

Preheat oven to 425°F. Transfer Wellington to a foil-lined baking sheet. Brush all over with beaten egg. Score a decorative pattern in pastry with a paring knife. Sprinkle liberally with coarse salt.

Bake until pastry is golden brown and center registers 110°F for rare or 120°F for medium-rare, 35-45 minutes. Rest 10 minutes.

Loosen from foil with a thin spatula, transfer to a carving board. Slice off ends, carve, and sprinkle cut surfaces with chives and more coarse salt.

---

Center-cut tenderloin is also sold as Chateaubriand. Substitute foie gras pâté for fresh foie gras — if using pâté, skip the foie gras searing and fat addition in Step 2 and spread pâté directly over the tenderloin during assembly in Step 3. Substitute spicy brown or hot English mustard for the Dijon. Any combination of button, cremini, shiitake, or portobello mushrooms works. Substitute other brandy or a barrel-aged spirit such as bourbon for the Cognac. Use high-quality all-butter puff pastry such as Dufour for best results.
