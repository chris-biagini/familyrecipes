# Caramelized Onion and Gruyère Biscuits

Worth the caramelization time.

Makes: 10 biscuits
Category: Baking

## Caramelize onions.

- Butter (unsalted), 1 tbsp
- Olive oil, 1 tbsp
- Onions, 2 small: Halved and thinly sliced.

Melt butter with oil in a large skillet over medium. Add onions, reduce to low, cover and steam 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove lid; cook, stirring occasionally, until deep brown, 10-20 more minutes. Cool completely.

## Make dough.

- Flour (all-purpose), 2 3/4 cups (345 g)
- Sugar (white), 1 tbsp (15 g)
- Baking powder, 2 tsp
- Baking soda, 1 tsp
- Salt (kosher), 3/4 tsp
- Butter (unsalted), 8 tbsp: Cold, cut into 1/2-inch pieces.
- Gruyère, 4 oz (115 g): Cut into 1/2-inch cubes.
- Buttermilk, 1 cup

Whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Cut in butter by hand or food processor until crumbly with pea-sized pieces. Stir in cheese. Combine buttermilk with cooled onions, then add to flour mixture. Stir and knead briefly in the bowl until just combined; a few floury spots are fine.

## Shape and bake.

- Salt (flaky)
- Black pepper

Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Turn dough onto a floured surface and roll to 1-inch thickness. Stamp out rounds with a floured 3-inch cutter; space apart on a parchment-lined sheet. Re-roll scraps as needed. Sprinkle with flaky salt and pepper. Bake 20-23 minutes until deep golden-brown with bubbly cheese around the edges.

---

Best warm on day one; reheat on day two. Substitute any Swiss-style cheese for the gruyère.

Based on a recipe from Alyce Shields at Pronto by Bar Bambino, via Tasting Table.
