# Cubano

Inspired by the movie *Chef*, this Cubano includes layers of smoked ham, roasted pork, melty Swiss cheese, and briny pickles.

Serves: 6
Category: Mains
Tags: american, comfort-food

- Pork (roasted), 1 1/2 cups (12 oz): Very thinly sliced.
- Ham (deli, smoked), 18 slices (18 oz)
- Cuban bread, 3 loaves (8 oz each): Split.
- Swiss cheese, 12 slices
- Pickle (dill), 12 slices: Very thin, lengthwise slices.
- Mustard (yellow), 1/2 cup
- Butter, 12 tbsp: Melted.

Heat a cast-iron skillet or griddle over medium-high heat until very hot. Working in batches as needed, cook pork and ham in the hot skillet, turning halfway through, until warmed and slightly crispy, about 2 minutes. Remove from skillet; keep warm.

Return skillet to medium-high heat. Cut each bread loaf in half crosswise. Working in batches as needed, toast bread in the hot skillet, cut sides down, about 1 minute. Divide pork and ham evenly among bottom roll halves; top each with 2 Swiss cheese slices and 2 pickle slices. Generously spread roll tops with mustard; add to sandwiches, mustard side down.

Brush skillet with 2 tablespoons melted butter. Add 2 sandwiches, bottom sides down; brush tops of sandwiches with 2 Tbsp. melted butter. Weight down with a heavy skillet, pressing slightly. Toast sandwiches, turning halfway through, until bread is golden and cheese is melted, 6 to 8 minutes. Repeat with remaining sandwiches and butter.

---

Or pulled pork (12 ounces) in place of roasted pork.

To tell if the skillet or griddle is hot and ready: Sprinkle a little water on the surface; it should immediately sizzle and evaporate.

Prep time: 20 mins. Cook time: 20 mins. Total time: 40 mins.

**Roasted Pork, Cuban-Style**

To make your own roasted pork, combine 3 tablespoons orange juice, 3 tablespoons lime juice, 4 teaspoons minced fresh garlic, 2 teaspoons mirin, 1 teaspoon cumin, 1 teaspoon dried oregano, 1 teaspoon ground coriander, 1/2 teaspoon salt, and 1/2 teaspoon black pepper in a gallon-size zip-top plastic bag set in a large bowl. Add a 1-pound boneless pork loin roast. Seal bag; turn to coat pork with marinade. Marinate in refrigerator at least 2 hours (or up to 24 hours). Remove pork from marinade. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Put pork on a rack set in a roasting pan. Roast pork 1 to 1 1/2 hours or until an instant-read thermometer inserted into thickest part registers 145°F (62°C). Transfer pork to a cutting board. Tent with foil and let stand 15 minutes. Cover tightly and chill overnight for easier slicing. Makes about 12 ounces cooked meat.
