# Beef Wellington

Serves: 6
Category: Mains
Tags: holiday

## Sear beef; coat with mustard rub.

- Beef tenderloin (center-cut), about 2 lbs
- Salt
- Black pepper
- Vegetable oil, 2 tbsp
- Horseradish (prepared), 2 tbsp
- Mustard (Dijon), 1 tbsp

Tie tenderloin at 1-inch intervals with butcher's twine. Season liberally with salt and pepper. Heat oil in a cast iron or stainless steel skillet over high heat until smoking. Sear, turning, until well browned on all sides, about 10 minutes total. Transfer to a plate.

Mix horseradish, mustard, and 1 tsp black pepper. Remove twine and rub mixture all over tenderloin. Refrigerate uncovered.

## Make duxelles; sear foie gras.

- Mushrooms, 1 lb: Roughly chopped.
- Butter (unsalted), 4 tbsp
- Shallots, 2: Finely diced.
- Thyme, 2 tsp: Finely minced.
- Cognac, 1/2 cup
- Heavy cream, 1/2 cup
- Soy sauce, 2 tsp
- Foie gras (fresh), 4 oz
- Vegetable oil, 1/2 tsp

Pulse mushrooms in a food processor in two batches until very finely chopped, about 10 short pulses per batch.

Melt butter in a 12-inch skillet over medium-high. Cook mushrooms, stirring occasionally, until liquid evaporates and they start to sizzle, about 8 minutes. Continue until browned with fond on the pan bottom, about 4 more minutes. Add shallots and thyme; cook until softened, about 2 minutes. Add cognac, scraping up fond, and cook until nearly dry, about 4 minutes. Add cream and soy sauce; cook, stirring, until mixture is thick and collects in one mass when the pan is shaken. Season with salt and pepper. Transfer to a bowl and refrigerate.

Season foie gras with salt and pepper. Heat oil in a small cast iron or stainless steel skillet over high heat until smoking. Sear foie gras without moving until well browned, about 30 seconds per side. Transfer to a paper-towel-lined plate. Pour rendered fat into duxelles and stir to combine. Refrigerate duxelles. Split each piece of foie gras in half horizontally; refrigerate. Chill all components at least 30 minutes.

## Assemble roll.

- Phyllo dough, 1 sheet
- Prosciutto, 1/4 lb: Sliced paper thin.

Lay a double layer of plastic wrap (about 2 feet long, 1 foot wide) on a cutting board. Lay phyllo on top. Shingle prosciutto in an even overlapping layer, leaving a 2-inch border along the top and bottom edges. Spread duxelles evenly over prosciutto.

Place tenderloin along the bottom edge; arrange foie gras evenly over the top. Roll tightly, using the plastic wrap to help compress. Rewrap in fresh plastic wrap, twisting the ends to secure. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes.

## Wrap in pastry.

- Flour
- Puff pastry, 14 oz: Thawed.
- Egg, 1: Beaten.

On a lightly floured surface, roll puff pastry into a rectangle at least 4 inches wider than the beef roll on its short side. Unwrap beef roll and place along the bottom edge, foie gras side up. Brush the 6 inches of pastry just above the roll with beaten egg. Roll beef in pastry until fully wrapped, seam on bottom, foie gras side up. Trim excess with a sharp knife.

Fold in the side flaps, fold down the top flaps, and trim the bottom flaps. Flip seam-side up, fold up the end flaps to seal, and return right-side up. Transfer to a plate and refrigerate at least 30 minutes.

## Bake and serve.

- Sea salt (coarse)
- Chives, 1 bunch: Finely minced.

Preheat oven to 425°F. Transfer Wellington to a foil-lined baking sheet. Brush all over with remaining beaten egg. Score a decorative pattern with a paring knife. Sprinkle liberally with coarse salt.

Bake until pastry is golden brown and center registers 110°F for rare or 120°F for medium-rare, 35-45 minutes. Rest 10 minutes.

Loosen from foil with a thin spatula and transfer to a carving board. Slice off ends, carve, and sprinkle cut surfaces with chives and more coarse salt.

---

Center-cut beef tenderloin is also known as Châteaubriand. For best results, use a high-quality all-butter puff pastry such as Dufour. Substitute spicy brown or hot English mustard for the Dijon. Any brandy or barrel-aged spirit (e.g., bourbon) works in place of Cognac. Button, cremini, shiitake, portobello, or a mix all work for the mushrooms. Maldon or fleur de sel recommended for the coarse salt. Foie gras pâté may be substituted for fresh foie gras — skip the searing step and spread pâté directly over the tenderloin during assembly.
