# Good Eats Beef Stew

Serves: 4
Category: Mains

## Braise the ribs.

- Tomato paste, 1/4 cup
- Apple cider vinegar, 1/4 cup
- Worcestershire sauce, 1 1/2 tsp
- Paprika, 1 tbsp
- Dried herbs, 1 1/2 tsp
- Short ribs (English-cut), 3 lbs
- Salt (kosher), 1 tbsp

Whisk together tomato paste, vinegar, Worcestershire, paprika, and dried herbs. Set aside.

Season ribs with salt. Heat a griddle over medium-high until a water drop bounces. Sear until browned on all sides. Toss in paste mixture to coat.

Wrap tightly in heavy-duty aluminum foil, place in a metal pan, and set in a cold oven on the middle rack. Set oven to 250°F and cook 4 hours.

## Degrease the liquid.

Remove from oven and carefully poke a hole in the foil pack over a heatproof container to drain the liquid. Refrigerate until fat separates, about 1 hour, then transfer to freezer until fat cap solidifies, about 1 more hour. Let ribs rest at room temperature.

## Cook vegetables; finish and serve.

- Onion, 1 large: Thinly sliced.
- Salt (kosher), 1 tsp
- Potatoes (red), 1 lb: Unpeeled, sliced.
- Black pepper
- Parsley, 1 tbsp: Chopped.

Remove fat cap from chilled liquid. Measure 1 tbsp fat into a large saucier over medium heat; reserve the rest for another use. Once fat melts, add onion and salt, stir to separate into rings, and cook 2-3 minutes. Add potatoes and a pinch of pepper, stir, then add reserved braising liquid. Cover tightly, reduce to low, and cook until potatoes are fork-tender, about 30 minutes.

Meanwhile, cut meat from bone, discarding connective tissue. Once potatoes are tender, uncover, set meat atop vegetables, cover, and cook 10 more minutes. Serve sprinkled with parsley.

---

Dried herbs: any combination of thyme, oregano, and rosemary.

Based on a recipe from Good Eats.
