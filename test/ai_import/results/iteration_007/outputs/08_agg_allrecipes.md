# Cubano

From the food truck scene in 'Chef'.

Serves: 6
Category: Mains

## Advance prep: marinate and roast pork.

- Orange juice, 3 tbsp
- Lime juice, 3 tbsp
- Garlic, 4 tsp: Minced.
- Mirin, 2 tsp
- Cumin, 1 tsp
- Oregano, 1 tsp
- Coriander, 1 tsp
- Salt, 1/2 tsp
- Black pepper, 1/2 tsp
- Pork loin roast (boneless), 1 lb

Combine juices, garlic, mirin, and spices in a gallon zip-top bag. Add pork, seal, and turn to coat. Marinate in refrigerator 2-24 hours.

Remove pork from marinade. Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Roast on a rack in a roasting pan until internal temp reaches 145°F (62°C), 1-1 1/2 hours. Tent with foil and rest 15 minutes. Refrigerate overnight for easier slicing, then slice very thin.

## Sear meat; toast bread.

- Ham (smoked), 18 slices (18 oz)
- Cuban bread, 3 loaves (8 oz each)

Heat a cast-iron skillet or griddle over medium-high until very hot. Working in batches, cook pork and ham, turning once, until warmed and slightly crispy, about 2 minutes. Keep warm.

Cut each loaf in half crosswise. Working in batches, toast bread cut-side down in same skillet, about 1 minute.

## Assemble and press.

- Swiss cheese, 12 slices
- Dill pickle, 12 slices: Very thin, cut lengthwise.
- Yellow mustard, 1/2 cup
- Butter, 12 tbsp: Melted.

Divide pork and ham among bottom bread halves. Top each with 2 cheese slices and 2 pickle slices. Spread mustard generously on top halves; close sandwiches mustard-side down.

Brush skillet with 2 tbsp butter. Add 2 sandwiches bottom-side down; brush tops with 2 tbsp butter. Weight with a heavy skillet. Toast 6-8 minutes, turning halfway, until bread is golden and cheese is melted. Repeat with remaining sandwiches and butter.

---

Substitute 12 oz (1 1/2 cups) pulled pork for the roasted pork — skip the advance prep step.

To test if the skillet is ready: sprinkle a few drops of water on the surface — they should immediately sizzle and evaporate.

Based on a recipe from Jaelyn Luong, Allrecipes.
