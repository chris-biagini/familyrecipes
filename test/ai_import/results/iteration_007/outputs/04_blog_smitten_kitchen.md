# Caramelized Onion and Gruyère Biscuits

Makes: 10 biscuits
Category: Baking
Tags: freezer-friendly

## Caramelize onions.

- Butter (unsalted), 1 tbsp
- Olive oil, 1 tbsp
- Onions, 2 small: Halved and thinly sliced.

Melt butter with olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onions, reduce to low, cover, and steam 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove lid and cook, stirring occasionally, until deep brown, 10-20 minutes more. Cool.

## Make dough; shape and bake.

- Flour (all-purpose), 2 3/4 cups
- Sugar (white), 1 tbsp
- Baking powder, 2 tsp
- Baking soda, 1 tsp
- Salt, 3/4 tsp
- Butter (unsalted), 8 tbsp: Cold, diced into 1/2-inch pieces.
- Gruyère, 4 oz (115 g): Cut into 1/2-inch cubes.
- Buttermilk, 1 cup
- Salt (flaky)
- Black pepper

Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment.

Combine flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Cut in cold butter by hand or food processor until crumbly with pieces no larger than a pea. Stir in cheese. Pour buttermilk over cooled onions, stir to combine, then add to flour mixture. Stir to combine — dough will be shaggy; knead briefly in the bowl until it holds. A few floury spots are fine.

Turn onto a floured surface and roll to 1-inch thick. Stamp out 3-inch rounds, re-rolling scraps as needed. Space on prepared sheet. Sprinkle with flaky salt and pepper.

Bake until deep golden-brown and cheese is bubbly at the edges, 20-23 minutes.

---

Substitute another Swiss-style cheese for the Gruyère. Coarse or kosher salt for the dough. Freeze unbaked; thaw briefly before baking. Best on day one; re-warm on day two.

Based on a recipe from Alyce Shields at the late Pronto by Bar Bambino, SF, via Tasting Table.
