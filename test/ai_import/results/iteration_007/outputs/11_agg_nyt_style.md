# No-Knead Bread

No effort, just time.

Makes: 1 loaf
Category: Bread

## Mix dough; rest.

- Flour (all-purpose), 3 1/3 cups (430 g): Plus more for dusting.
- Yeast (instant), generous 1/4 tsp (1 g)
- Salt (kosher), 2 tsp (8 g)
- Water, 1 1/2 cups (345 g)

Combine flour, yeast, and salt. Add water and stir until blended — dough will be shaggy and sticky. Cover with plastic wrap and rest at least 12 hours, preferably 18, at about 70°F. Dough is ready when surface is dotted with bubbles.

## Shape; second rise.

- Cornmeal

Flour a work surface, turn dough onto it, sprinkle with a little more flour, and fold over once or twice. Cover loosely and rest 15 minutes.

Shape into a ball using just enough flour to prevent sticking. Generously coat a cotton towel (not terry cloth) with flour or cornmeal; place dough seam side down on towel and dust top. Cover with another towel and let rise about 2 hours — dough will more than double and won't spring back when poked.

## Bake.

About 30 minutes before dough is ready, heat oven to 450°F with a 6-8 quart heavy covered pot (cast iron, enamel, Pyrex, or ceramic) inside. When dough is ready, carefully remove pot. Turn dough into pot seam side up — it may look messy, that's fine. Shake once or twice if uneven. Cover and bake 30 minutes, then remove lid and bake another 15-30 minutes until beautifully browned. Cool on a rack.

---

Can substitute bread flour for all-purpose. Use wheat bran in place of cornmeal.

Based on a recipe from Jim Lahey, adapted by Mark Bittman.
