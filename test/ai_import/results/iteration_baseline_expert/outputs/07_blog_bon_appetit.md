# Popover-Topped Pot Pie

Pot pie, upgraded.

Serves: 4
Category: Mains
Tags: vegetarian, baked

## Make the filling.

- Olive oil (extra-virgin), 1/4 cup plus 1 tbsp
- Potatoes (Yukon Gold), 12 oz: Peeled, cut into 1/2" pieces.
- Asparagus, 1 bunch: Trimmed, cut into 1/2" pieces.
- Carrots, 2: Peeled, halved lengthwise, cut into 1/4"-thick half-moons.
- Celery, 2 stalks: Thinly sliced.
- Shallots, 2: Finely chopped.
- Peas (frozen), 1 cup
- Garlic, 6 cloves: Finely grated.
- Flour (all-purpose), 1/4 cup (31 g)
- Broth (vegetable, low-sodium), 2 cups
- White wine (dry), 1/2 cup
- Heavy cream, 2/3 cup
- Dijon mustard, 1-2 tbsp
- Lemon zest, 1 1/2 tsp: Finely grated.
- Dill (fresh), 1/3 cup: Finely chopped.
- Salt (Diamond Crystal kosher), 1 1/2 tsp
- Black pepper, 3/4 tsp

Heat oil in large (≥14-cup) high-sided ovenproof skillet over medium. Add potatoes; cook stirring often, 2 minutes. Add asparagus, carrots, celery, shallots, and peas; cook stirring often until potatoes are just shy of fork-tender, 15-18 minutes.

Add garlic and flour; stir until vegetables are coated and no dry flour remains. Stirring constantly, gradually add broth and wine, then stir in cream, mustard, lemon zest, dill, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer and cook until sauce coats a spoon, about 1 minute. Remove from heat; taste and adjust mustard, salt, and pepper. Let rest at least 20 minutes and up to 1 hour to thicken — don't stir; a slight skin should form.

## Make the batter and bake.

- Eggs, 5
- Parmesan, 1 oz: Finely grated.
- Whole milk, 1 1/3 cups
- Flour (all-purpose), 1 1/4 cups (156 g)
- Baking powder, 1/2 tsp
- Salt (Diamond Crystal kosher), 1 tsp
- Black pepper, 1/4 tsp
- Dill (fresh), 2 tbsp: Finely chopped.

Preheat oven to 425°F, rack in middle.

Blend eggs on high speed until light, fluffy, and noticeably increased in volume. Add Parmesan, milk, baking powder, flour, salt, and pepper; blend on medium just until smooth. Stir in dill. Cover and let sit until filling is ready.

Pour batter over filling, directing flow over an inverted spoon to distribute gently without disturbing the surface. Bake 45-55 minutes until topping is deep golden brown and very puffed.

---

Morton kosher salt equivalents: 1 tsp in filling, 1/2 tsp in batter.

Mustard amount: start with 1 tbsp, add up to 1 tbsp more after removing from heat.
