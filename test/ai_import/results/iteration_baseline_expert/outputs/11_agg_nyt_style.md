# No-Knead Bread

Patience is the only technique.

Makes: 1 loaf
Category: Bread
Tags: vegan, make-ahead, baked

## Mix the dough.

- Flour (all-purpose), 430 g
- Yeast (instant), 1 g
- Salt (kosher), 8 g
- Water, 345 g

Combine flour, yeast, and salt in large bowl. Add water; stir until blended — dough will be shaggy and sticky. Cover and let rest 12-18 hours at about 70°F until surface is dotted with bubbles.

## Shape and proof.

- Cornmeal

Turn dough onto lightly floured surface; fold once or twice. Cover loosely and rest 15 minutes.

Quickly shape into a ball, using just enough flour to prevent sticking. Generously coat a cotton (not terry cloth) towel with cornmeal; place dough seam-side down and dust top. Cover with second towel and let rise about 2 hours until more than doubled and dough doesn't spring back when poked.

## Bake.

At least 30 minutes before dough is ready, heat oven to 450°F with 6-8 qt heavy covered pot inside. Remove pot; drop dough in seam-side up. If uneven, shake once or twice — straightens as it bakes. Cover and bake 30 minutes. Uncover and bake another 15-30 minutes until deeply browned. Cool on rack.

---

Imperial equivalents: 3 1/3 cups flour, generous 1/4 tsp yeast, 2 tsp salt, 1 1/2 cups water.
Bread flour can substitute for all-purpose. Wheat bran or flour can substitute for cornmeal when coating the towel.
Recipe from Jim Lahey (Sullivan Street Bakery), adapted by Mark Bittman.
