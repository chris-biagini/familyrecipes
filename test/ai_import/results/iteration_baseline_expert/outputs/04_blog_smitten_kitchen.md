# Caramelized Onion and Gruyère Biscuits

Worth the wait on those onions.

Makes: 10 biscuits
Category: Baking
Tags: baked, vegetarian

## Caramelize the onions.

- Butter (unsalted), 1 tbsp
- Olive oil, 1 tbsp
- Onions, 2 small: Halved and thinly sliced.

Melt butter with olive oil in a large skillet over medium. Add onions, reduce to low, cover, and let steam about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Uncover; cook until deep brown, 10-20 minutes more. Cool completely.

## Make the biscuits.

- Butter (unsalted), 8 tbsp: Cold, cut into 1/2-inch pieces.
- Flour (all-purpose), 345 g
- Sugar (white), 15 g
- Baking powder, 2 tsp
- Baking soda, 1 tsp
- Salt, 3/4 tsp: Coarse or kosher.
- Gruyère, 115 g: Cut into 1/2-inch cubes.
- Buttermilk, 1 cup
- Flaky sea salt
- Black pepper

Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line baking sheet with parchment.

Combine flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Cut in butter until crumbly with pea-sized pieces — by hand with fingertips or a pastry blender, or pulse in food processor then transfer to bowl. Stir in cheese. Stir buttermilk into cooled onions, then add to flour mixture and stir until combined. Dough will seem dry — use hands to bring it together. A few floury spots are fine.

Turn onto floured surface, roll to 1-inch thickness. Stamp out rounds with a floured 3-inch cutter; space on prepared sheet. Re-roll scraps as needed. Sprinkle with flaky sea salt and black pepper. Bake until deep golden-brown and cheese is bubbly at edges, 20-23 minutes. Eat warm.

---

Adapted from Alyce Shields at the late Pronto by Bar Bambino, San Francisco, via Tasting Table.

Substitute another Swiss-style cheese for the Gruyère. Best day-of; reheat on day two. Can freeze unbaked biscuits; thaw before baking.

Imperial equivalents: 9 tbsp butter total (1 tbsp for onions, 8 tbsp for dough), 2 3/4 cups flour, 1 tbsp sugar, 4 oz gruyère.
