# Beef-and-Bean Chili

Lots of beef, two kinds of beans.

Serves: 6
Category: Mains
Tags: comfort-food, american

## Brown and simmer.

- Ground beef, 4 lb
- Garlic, 6 cloves: Minced.
- Tomato sauce, 2 cans (8 oz each)
- Tomato paste, 1 can (6 oz)
- Chili powder, 1/4 cup
- Cumin, 2 tsp
- Oregano, 2 tsp
- Salt (kosher), 2 tsp
- Cayenne, 1/2 tsp

Brown ground beef with garlic in large heavy pot until cooked through. Drain off most of the fat, leaving a little. Add tomato sauce, paste, chili powder, cumin, oregano, salt, and cayenne; stir to combine. Cover and simmer on low 1 hour, stirring occasionally. Add up to 1 1/2 cups water if chili runs low.

## Add beans and thicken.

- Beans (kidney and pinto), 4 cans (15 oz each): Drained and rinsed.
- Masa harina, 1/2 cup
- Cheddar
- Bacon
- Jalapeños

Stir beans into chili. Whisk masa harina with 1/2 cup water until smooth, stir into chili. Simmer 10 minutes. Adjust seasoning. Top with cheddar, bacon, and jalapeños.

---

Substitute cornmeal for masa harina. Cheddar (shredded), bacon (crumbled), and jalapeños (sliced) are for topping. Rinse canned beans — they can be slimy right out of the can.

By Ree Drummond.
