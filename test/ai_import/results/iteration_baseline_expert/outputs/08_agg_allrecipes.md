# Cubano

Pressed, crispy, cheesy — the movie got it right.

Serves: 6
Category: Mains

## Cook the meats.

- Roasted pork, 1 1/2 cups (12 oz): Very thinly sliced.
- Ham (deli smoked), 18 slices (18 oz)

Heat cast-iron skillet or griddle over medium-high until very hot. Cook pork and ham in batches, turning once, until warmed and slightly crispy, about 2 minutes. Keep warm.

## Assemble and press.

- Cuban bread, 3 loaves (8 oz each): Split.
- Swiss cheese, 12 slices
- Dill pickle, 12 slices: Very thin, lengthwise.
- Yellow mustard, 1/2 cup
- Butter, 12 tbsp: Melted.

Return skillet to medium-high. Cut each loaf in half crosswise. Toast bread cut-side down about 1 minute. Divide pork and ham among bottom halves; top each with 2 cheese slices and 2 pickle slices. Spread tops generously with mustard; close sandwiches.

Brush skillet with 2 tbsp butter. Add 2 sandwiches; brush tops with 2 tbsp butter. Weight down with a heavy skillet. Toast, turning once, until golden and cheese is melted, 6-8 minutes. Repeat with remaining sandwiches and butter.

---

Inspired by the film *Chef*. Recipe by Jaelyn Luong, Allrecipes Test Kitchen.

Can substitute pulled pork for the roasted pork.

**Cuban-style roasted pork:** Combine 3 tbsp orange juice, 3 tbsp lime juice, 4 tsp minced garlic, 2 tsp mirin, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp dried oregano, 1 tsp coriander, 1/2 tsp salt, and 1/2 tsp black pepper in a zip-top bag. Add 1 lb boneless pork loin roast; seal and marinate refrigerated 2-24 hours. Roast on rack at 350°F (175°C) until thermometer reads 145°F (62°C), 1-1 1/2 hours. Tent with foil, rest 15 minutes. Chill overnight for easier slicing. Makes about 12 oz.
