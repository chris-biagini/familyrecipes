# Good Eats Beef Stew

Worth the six hours.

Serves: 4
Category: Mains
Tags: comfort-food, american

## Sear and braise.

- Tomato paste, 1/4 cup
- Apple cider vinegar, 1/4 cup
- Worcestershire sauce, 1 1/2 tsp
- Paprika, 1 tbsp
- Dried herbs, 1 1/2 tsp
- Short ribs (English-cut), 3 lbs
- Salt (kosher), 1 tbsp

Whisk together tomato paste, vinegar, Worcestershire, paprika, and dried herbs. Set aside.

Season ribs with salt. Sear on a griddle over medium-high heat until browned on all sides. Toss with paste mixture. Seal tightly in heavy-duty aluminum foil. Place packet in a metal pan and set in a cold oven on the middle rack. Set to 250°F and cook 4 hours.

## Degrease braising liquid.

Remove from oven. Carefully poke a hole in the foil over a heatproof container and drain liquid. Refrigerate about 1 hour until fat begins to separate, then transfer to freezer until fat cap solidifies, about 1 more hour. Let ribs rest at room temperature while liquid cools.

## Cook vegetables; finish.

- Onion, 1: Thinly sliced.
- Salt (kosher), 1 tsp
- Potatoes (red), 1 lb: Unpeeled and sliced.
- Black pepper
- Parsley, 1 tbsp: Chopped.

Remove and reserve fat cap. Add 1 tbsp of the fat to a large saucier over medium heat. Once melted, add onion and 1 tsp salt; cook 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add potatoes and a pinch of black pepper, then pour in braising liquid. Cover tightly, reduce to low, and cook until potatoes are fork-tender, about 30 minutes.

Cut meat from bone, discarding connective tissue. Once potatoes are tender, set meat atop vegetables, cover, and cook 10 more minutes. Serve sprinkled with parsley.

---

Dried herbs can be any combination of thyme, oregano, and rosemary. Reserve remaining fat from the cap for another use. From Alton Brown's *Good Eats*.
