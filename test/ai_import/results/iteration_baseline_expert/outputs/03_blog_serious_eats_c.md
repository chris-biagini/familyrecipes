# Beef Wellington

Worth every step.

Serves: 6
Category: Mains
Tags: baked, holiday

## Sear and season the beef.

- Beef tenderloin (center-cut), about 2 lbs: Trimmed of silverskin and fat.
- Salt
- Black pepper
- Vegetable oil, 2 tbsp
- Horseradish (prepared), 2 tbsp
- Mustard (Dijon), 1 tbsp

Tie tenderloin at 1-inch intervals with butcher's twine. Season liberally with salt and pepper.

Sear in oil in cast iron or stainless skillet over high heat until browned on all sides, about 10 minutes total. Transfer to plate.

Once cool enough to handle, remove twine. Mix horseradish, mustard, and 1 tsp black pepper; rub over all surfaces. Refrigerate uncovered.

## Make duxelles and sear foie gras.

- Mushrooms, 1 lb: Roughly chopped.
- Butter (unsalted), 4 tbsp
- Shallots, 2 medium: Finely diced.
- Thyme (fresh), 2 tsp: Finely minced.
- Cognac, 1/2 cup
- Heavy cream, 1/2 cup
- Soy sauce, 2 tsp
- Foie gras (fresh), 4 oz
- Vegetable oil, 1/2 tsp

Pulse mushrooms in food processor until very finely chopped, working in two batches.

Melt butter in large skillet over medium-high. Cook mushrooms until liquid evaporates and browning begins, about 12 minutes. Add shallots and thyme; cook until softened, 2 minutes. Add cognac, scrape up browned bits, and cook until nearly dry, about 4 minutes. Add cream and soy sauce; cook until thick and collecting in one mass when pan is shaken. Season with salt and pepper. Refrigerate.

Season foie gras with salt and pepper. Sear in 1/2 tsp oil in small cast iron over high heat, about 30 seconds per side. Drain on paper towels. Pour rendered fat into duxelles; stir to combine. Halve each piece horizontally. Refrigerate all components at least 30 minutes.

## Assemble and wrap.

- Phyllo dough, 1 sheet
- Prosciutto, 1/4 lb: Sliced paper thin.
- Flour
- Puff pastry, 14 oz: Thawed.
- Egg, 1: Beaten.

Lay double layer of plastic wrap on cutting board. Place phyllo on top. Shingle prosciutto over phyllo, leaving a 2-inch border at top and bottom. Spread duxelles evenly over prosciutto.

Lay tenderloin along bottom edge; place foie gras slices evenly on top. Roll tightly from bottom, using plastic wrap to compress as you go. Re-wrap firmly, twisting ends. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes.

Dust surface with flour. Roll puff pastry into rectangle at least 4 inches wider than roll on short side. Unwrap beef roll; place along bottom edge of pastry with foie gras side up. Brush 6 inches of pastry above roll with beaten egg. Roll beef in pastry until fully wrapped, seam on bottom — foie gras side ends up on top. Trim excess. Fold end flaps to seal: sides in, then top down; trim bottom flaps; flip and fold up to seal. Return right-side up. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes.

## Bake.

- Sea salt (coarse)
- Chives, 1 bunch: Finely minced.

Preheat oven to 218°C, rack in center. Transfer Wellington to foil-lined baking sheet. Brush all over with remaining beaten egg. Score decorative pattern in pastry with a paring knife. Sprinkle liberally with coarse sea salt.

Bake until pastry is golden brown and center registers 43°C for rare or 49°C for medium-rare, 35-45 minutes. Rest 10 minutes.

Loosen from foil with metal spatula; transfer to carving board. Trim and discard ends. Carve; sprinkle cut surfaces with chives and more coarse salt.

---

Center-cut tenderloin is also known as Châteaubriand.

Use high-quality all-butter puff pastry — Dufour is ideal.

Any mushroom variety works: button, cremini, shiitake, portobello, or a mix.

Canola oil can substitute for vegetable oil.

Cognac substitute: any brandy or barrel-aged spirit such as bourbon.

Mustard alternatives: spicy brown or hot English mustard.

**Foie gras pate substitution:** Skip the foie gras searing step. During assembly, spread pate on top of tenderloin in place of sliced seared foie gras.

Imperial temperature equivalents: 425°F oven; 110°F for rare, 120°F for medium-rare.

Special equipment: butcher's twine, instant-read thermometer.
