# No-Knead Bread

Very little effort — only time.

Makes: 1 loaf
Category: Bread

## Mix dough; rest overnight.

- Flour (all-purpose), 3 1/3 cups
- Yeast (instant), generous 1/4 tsp
- Salt (kosher), 2 tsp
- Water, 1 1/2 cups

Combine flour, yeast, and salt in a large bowl. Add water and stir until blended — dough will be shaggy and sticky. Cover with plastic wrap and rest at least 12 hours, preferably 18, at about 70°F, until surface is dotted with bubbles.

## Shape; proof.

- Cornmeal

Lightly flour a work surface, turn dough out, sprinkle with a bit more flour, and fold once or twice. Cover loosely and rest 15 minutes.

Using minimal flour to prevent sticking, quickly shape into a ball. Generously coat a cotton (not terry cloth) towel with cornmeal; place dough seam side down and dust top with more cornmeal. Cover with a second cotton towel and let rise about 2 hours, until more than doubled and not springing back when poked.

## Bake.

At least 30 minutes before dough is ready, preheat oven to 450°F with a 6-8 qt heavy covered pot (cast iron, enamel, Pyrex, or ceramic) inside. When ready, carefully remove pot, slide hand under towel, and turn dough into pot seam side up — it may look like a mess; shake once or twice to redistribute. Cover and bake 30 minutes, then remove lid and bake another 15-30 minutes until beautifully browned. Cool on rack.

---

Substitute bread flour for all-purpose. Substitute wheat bran for cornmeal.

Based on a recipe from Jim Lahey, adapted by Mark Bittman for *The New York Times*.
