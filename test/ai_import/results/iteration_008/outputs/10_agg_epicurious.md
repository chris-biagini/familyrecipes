# Good Eats Beef Stew

Serves: 4
Category: Mains

## Make paste; sear and braise ribs.

- Tomato paste, 1/4 cup
- Apple cider vinegar, 1/4 cup
- Worcestershire sauce, 1 1/2 tsp
- Paprika, 1 tbsp
- Dried herbs, 1 1/2 tsp: Any combination of thyme, oregano, and rosemary.
- Short ribs (English-cut), 3 lbs
- Salt (kosher), 1 tbsp

Whisk together tomato paste, vinegar, Worcestershire, paprika, and herbs. Season ribs with 1 tbsp salt. Sear on a griddle over medium-high heat until browned on all sides. Toss ribs in paste, wrap tightly in heavy-duty foil, and place in a metal pan. Put in a cold oven on the middle rack, set to 250°F, and cook 4 hours.

Remove from oven, carefully pierce foil over a heatproof container, and drain braising liquid. Refrigerate until fat separates, about 1 hour, then transfer to freezer until fat cap solidifies, about 1 hour more. Leave ribs at room temperature while liquid cools.

## Cook vegetables; finish and serve.

- Onion, 1: Thinly sliced.
- Potatoes (red), 1 lb: Unpeeled, sliced.
- Salt (kosher), 1 tsp
- Black pepper, a pinch
- Parsley, 1 tbsp: Chopped.

Remove fat cap from braising liquid; measure out 1 tbsp of fat. Melt in a large saucier over medium heat. Add onion and 1 tsp salt; cook 2-3 minutes, stirring to break into rings. Add potatoes and black pepper. Pour in braising liquid, cover, reduce to low, and cook until potatoes are fork-tender, about 30 minutes.

Meanwhile, cut meat from bone, discarding connective tissue. Once potatoes are tender, set meat atop vegetables, cover, and cook 10 more minutes. Serve sprinkled with parsley.

---

Reserve the remaining fat skimmed from the braising liquid for another use.
