# Cubano

From the movie *Chef*.

Serves: 6
Category: Mains

## Advance prep: make roasted pork.

- Orange juice, 3 tbsp
- Lime juice, 3 tbsp
- Garlic, 4 tsp: Minced.
- Mirin, 2 tsp
- Cumin, 1 tsp
- Oregano (dried), 1 tsp
- Coriander, 1 tsp
- Salt, 1/2 tsp
- Black pepper, 1/2 tsp
- Pork loin roast (boneless), 1 lb

Combine juices, garlic, mirin, and spices in a gallon zip-top bag set in a bowl. Add pork, seal, and turn to coat. Marinate in refrigerator at least 2 hours, up to 24.

Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Set pork on a rack in a roasting pan. Roast 1 to 1 1/2 hours until an instant-read thermometer reads 145°F (62°C). Tent with foil and rest 15 minutes. Cover tightly and chill overnight for easier slicing. Makes about 12 oz.

## Cook meats; toast bread.

- Pork (roasted), 1 1/2 cups (12 oz): Very thinly sliced.
- Ham (deli smoked), 18 slices (18 oz)
- Bread (Cuban), 3 loaves (8 oz each): Split.

Heat a cast-iron skillet or griddle over medium-high until very hot. Working in batches, cook pork and ham, turning once, until warmed and slightly crispy, about 2 minutes. Keep warm. Cut each loaf in half crosswise; toast cut-side down in skillet, about 1 minute.

## Assemble and press.

- Swiss cheese, 12 slices
- Pickle (dill), 12 slices: Very thin, lengthwise.
- Yellow mustard, 1/2 cup
- Butter, 12 tbsp: Melted.

Divide pork and ham evenly over bottom halves. Top each with 2 slices Swiss and 2 pickle slices. Spread tops generously with mustard; close sandwiches.

Brush skillet with 2 tbsp butter. Add 2 sandwiches; brush tops with 2 tbsp butter. Weight down with a heavy skillet, pressing slightly. Toast, turning once halfway, until golden and cheese is melted, 6-8 minutes. Repeat with remaining sandwiches and butter.

---

Substitute pulled pork for the roasted pork.

Based on a recipe from Allrecipes by Jaelyn Luong.
