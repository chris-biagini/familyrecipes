# Caramelized Onion and Gruyère Biscuits

Pretty much perfect.

Makes: 10 biscuits
Category: Baking

## Caramelize onions.

- Butter (unsalted), 1 tbsp
- Olive oil, 1 tbsp
- Onions, 2 small: Halved and thinly sliced.

Melt butter with olive oil in large skillet over medium. Add onions, reduce to low, cover, and steam 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Uncover and cook, stirring, until deep brown, 10-20 more minutes. Cool.

## Make dough; shape and bake.

- Flour (all-purpose), 2 3/4 cups (345 g)
- Sugar (white), 1 tbsp (15 g)
- Baking powder, 2 tsp
- Baking soda, 1 tsp
- Salt (kosher), 3/4 tsp
- Butter (unsalted), 8 tbsp: Cold, cut into 1/2-inch pieces.
- Gruyère, 4 oz (115 g): Cut into 1/2-inch cubes.
- Buttermilk, 1 cup
- Flaky sea salt
- Black pepper

Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment.

Whisk flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Work cold butter into flour by hand or food processor until crumbly with pea-sized pieces. Stir in cheese. Pour buttermilk over cooled onions; add to flour mixture and stir until combined. Dough will seem dry — knead a few times in bowl until it holds together; a few floury spots are fine.

Turn onto floured surface and roll to 1-inch thickness. Stamp out 3-inch circles and space on prepared sheet; re-roll scraps as needed. Sprinkle with flaky sea salt and pepper. Bake 20-23 minutes until deep golden-brown and cheese is melted and bubbly at edges. Eat warm.

---

Best on day one; re-warm on day two. Coarse salt works in place of kosher. Substitute another Swiss-style cheese for the gruyère. Buttermilk can be made at home.

Based on a recipe from Alyce Shields at the late Pronto by Bar Bambino in SF, via Tasting Table.
