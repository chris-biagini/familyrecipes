# Popover-Topped Pot Pie

Serves: 4
Category: Mains
Tags: vegetarian, comfort-food

- Olive oil (extra-virgin), 1/4 cup plus 1 tbsp
- Potatoes (Yukon Gold), 12 oz (about 2 large): Peeled, cut into 1/2" pieces.
- Asparagus, 1 bunch: Trimmed, cut into 1/2" pieces.
- Carrots (medium), 2: Peeled, halved lengthwise, cut into 1/4"-thick half-moons.
- Celery, 2 stalks: Thinly sliced.
- Shallots (medium), 2: Finely chopped.
- Peas (frozen), 1 cup
- Garlic, 6 cloves: Finely grated.
- Flour (all-purpose), 1 1/2 cups (187 g): Divided.
- Vegetable broth (low-sodium), 2 cups
- White wine (dry), 1/2 cup
- Heavy cream, 2/3 cup
- Mustard (Dijon), 1 tbsp: Or more.
- Lemon zest, 1 1/2 tsp: Finely grated.
- Dill, 1/3 cup plus 2 tbsp: Finely chopped, divided.
- Salt (kosher), 2 1/2 tsp: Divided, plus more.
- Pepper, 1 tsp: Freshly ground, divided, plus more.
- Eggs (large), 5
- Parmesan, 1 oz: Finely grated.
- Milk (whole), 1 1/3 cups
- Baking powder, 1/2 tsp

Heat 1/4 cup plus 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil in a large (14-cup capacity or more) high-sided ovenproof skillet, rondeau, or Dutch oven over medium. Add potatoes and cook, stirring often, 2 minutes. Add asparagus, carrots, celery, shallots, and peas and cook, stirring often, until potatoes are not quite fork-tender (a knife inserted into flesh should meet with a little resistance), 15-18 minutes.

Add garlic and 1/4 cup (31 g) all-purpose flour; stir until vegetables are coated and no pockets of dry flour remain. Stirring constantly, gradually pour in vegetable broth and white wine, then add heavy cream, 1 tbsp Dijon mustard, lemon zest, 1/3 cup finely chopped dill, 1 1/2 tsp Diamond Crystal or 1 tsp Morton kosher salt, and 3/4 tsp freshly ground pepper. Bring to a simmer and cook until sauce coats a spoon, about 1 minute. Remove from heat; add more mustard (up to 1 tbsp) and season with more salt and pepper if needed. Let sit at least 20 minutes and up to 1 hour to thicken. (Do not stir; you want a slight skin to form.)

Meanwhile, place a rack in middle of oven; preheat to 425°F. Blend eggs in a blender on high speed until light and fluffy. Add Parmesan, milk, baking powder, remaining 1 1/4 cups (156 g) all-purpose flour, 1 tsp Diamond Crystal or 1/2 tsp Morton kosher salt, and 1/4 tsp freshly ground pepper; blend on medium speed just until smooth. Stir in remaining 2 tbsp finely chopped dill. Tightly cover batter and let sit until filling is ready.

Holding a spoon upside down over filling, gradually pour batter over spoon to cover filling. Bake pot pie until topping is deep golden brown and very puffed, 45-55 minutes.

---

Total time: 1 hour 45 minutes.
