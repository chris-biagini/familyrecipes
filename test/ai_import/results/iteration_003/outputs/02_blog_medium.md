# Easy Weeknight Chicken Stir-Fry

Comes together in about 30 minutes.

Serves: 4
Category: Mains
Tags: weeknight, quick

## Marinate chicken.

- Chicken thighs (boneless, skinless), 1 1/2 lbs: Cut into bite-sized pieces.
- Soy sauce, 2 tbsp
- Rice vinegar, 1 tbsp
- Cornstarch, 1 tsp

Combine the chicken pieces with the soy sauce, rice vinegar, and cornstarch. Toss to coat evenly and let sit for at least 10 minutes while you prep the vegetables.

## Cook chicken.

- Vegetable oil, 1 tbsp

Heat the vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over high heat until it just begins to smoke. Add the chicken in a single layer and cook without stirring for 2-3 minutes until golden on the bottom. Stir and cook another 2 minutes until cooked through. Transfer chicken to a plate.

## Cook vegetables.

- Vegetable oil, 1 tbsp
- Red bell pepper, 1: Sliced into thin strips.
- Broccoli florets, 1 cup
- Carrots, 2 medium: Sliced on the bias.
- Green onions, 3: Cut into 1-inch pieces.
- Garlic, 3 cloves: Minced.
- Ginger (fresh), 1 tbsp: Minced.

Add the remaining tablespoon of oil to the wok. Add the bell pepper, broccoli, and carrots. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp. Add the green onions, garlic, and ginger and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.

## Finish stir-fry.

- Soy sauce, 3 tbsp
- Sesame oil, 1 tbsp
- Honey, 1 tbsp
- Sriracha, 1 tsp

Whisk together the soy sauce, sesame oil, honey, and sriracha in a small bowl. Return the chicken to the wok and pour the sauce over everything. Toss to coat and cook for 1-2 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly and everything is well coated.

Serve immediately over steamed rice.

---

Chicken breast can be substituted for thighs, but be careful not to overcook as breasts dry out faster. Tamari can be used in place of soy sauce for a gluten-free version.
