# Drop Biscuits

A simple biscuit that comes together in minutes. No rolling or cutting needed — just drop them onto the sheet and bake.

Makes: 12 biscuits

- Flour (all-purpose), 2 cups
- Baking powder, 1 tablespoon
- Salt, 1 teaspoon
- Sugar (white), 1/2 teaspoon
- Butter (cold), 1/3 cup: Cut into small pieces.
- Milk (whole), 3/4 cup
- Honey, 1 tablespoon

Preheat your oven to 425°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.

In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, salt, and sugar. Add the cold butter pieces and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to work the butter into the flour until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with some pea-sized pieces remaining. Pour in the milk and honey and stir with a fork just until the dough comes together. Do not overmix — a few lumps are fine.

Using a large spoon, drop roughly 1/4 cup mounds of dough onto the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2 inches apart. Bake for 12-14 minutes until the tops are golden brown. Serve warm with butter and jam.
