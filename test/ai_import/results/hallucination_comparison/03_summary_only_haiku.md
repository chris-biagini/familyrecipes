# Beef Stroganoff

A classic Russian dish featuring tender strips of beef in a rich, creamy mushroom sauce served over egg noodles.

Serves: 4

## Cook the beef and vegetables.

- Beef sirloin, 1 1/2 lbs: Cut into strips.
- Butter, 3 tbsp
- Onion, 1: Diced.
- Garlic, 2 cloves: Minced.
- Mushrooms, 8 oz: Sliced.
- Salt
- Black pepper
- Paprika, 1 tsp

Heat butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add beef strips and cook until browned, about 3-4 minutes per side. Remove beef and set aside.

Add onion and garlic to the same skillet and cook until softened, about 3-4 minutes. Add mushrooms and cook until they release their moisture and begin to brown, about 5 minutes. Season with salt, pepper, and paprika.

## Make the sauce.

- Beef broth, 1 1/2 cups
- Worcestershire sauce, 1 tbsp
- Sour cream, 1 cup

Add beef broth and Worcestershire sauce to the skillet, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom. Return beef to the skillet and simmer until the sauce reduces slightly, about 10 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in sour cream until combined. Do not boil after adding sour cream.

## Serve.

- Egg noodles, 12 oz: Cooked according to package directions.

Serve stroganoff over cooked egg noodles.

---

Adapted from Julia Child. The original recipe calls for beef sirloin, but beef chuck or round steak can be substituted. Some versions omit Worcestershire sauce or use red wine instead of beef broth.
