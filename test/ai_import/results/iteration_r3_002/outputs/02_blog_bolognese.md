# Spaghetti Bolognese

A quick 30-minute Bolognese with depth of flavor.

Serves: 5
Category: Mains

## Make the sauce.

- Olive oil, 1 1/2 tbsp
- Garlic, 2 cloves: Minced.
- Onion, 1: Finely chopped.
- Beef mince (ground beef), 500 g
- Red wine (dry), 125 ml
- Beef bouillon cubes, 2: Crumbled or granulated.
- Tomatoes (crushed, canned), 800 g
- Tomato paste, 2 tbsp
- Sugar (white), 2 tsp
- Worcestershire sauce, 2 tsp
- Bay leaves (dried), 2
- Thyme (fresh), 2 sprigs
- Salt, 3/4 tsp
- Black pepper, 1/2 tsp

Heat oil in a large pot or deep skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion and garlic; cook 3 minutes until light golden and softened.

Turn heat to high and add beef. Cook, breaking it up as you go, until browned.

Add red wine. Bring to simmer and cook 1 minute, scraping the bottom of the pot, until the alcohol smell is gone.

Add remaining ingredients. Stir, bring to a simmer then reduce heat to medium so it bubbles gently. Cook 20-30 minutes (no lid), adding water if sauce gets too thick. Stir occasionally.

Taste and add more salt if desired.

## Cook the pasta and serve.

- Spaghetti (dried), 400 g
- Parmesan
- Parsley: Finely chopped.

Bring large pot of salted water to boil. Add pasta and cook per packet directions minus 1 minute.

Scoop out a mug of pasta cooking water and set aside, then drain pasta.

Add pasta into bolognese sauce with about 125 ml reserved pasta water over medium heat. Toss gently 1 1/2-2 minutes until spaghetti turns red and sauce thickens.

Divide between bowls. Garnish with parmesan and parsley if desired.

---

Slow simmer option: add 3/4 cup water, cover with lid and simmer on very low for 2-2.5 hours, stirring every 30 minutes. Uncover and simmer 20 minutes to thicken sauce.

Substitute water or beef broth for the red wine. Substitute half pork mince for half the beef mince. Substitute tomato passata for the crushed tomatoes. Substitute 1/2 tsp dried thyme or oregano for the fresh thyme sprigs. Sugar is only needed if desired. Parmesan and parsley are optional garnish.
