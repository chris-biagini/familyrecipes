# Chewy Chocolate Chip Cookies

Makes: 16 cookies
Category: Baking

## Mix the dough.

- Flour (all-purpose), 281 g
- Baking soda, 1 tsp
- Cornstarch, 1 1/2 tsp
- Salt, 1/2 tsp
- Butter (unsalted), 170 g: Melted and cooled for 5 minutes.
- Sugar (brown), 150 g: Packed.
- Sugar (white), 100 g
- Egg, 1: Room temperature.
- Egg yolk, 1: Room temperature.
- Vanilla extract, 2 tsp
- Chocolate chips (semi-sweet), 225 g

In a large bowl, whisk the flour, baking soda, cornstarch, and salt together. Set aside.

In a medium bowl, whisk the melted butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar together until no lumps remain. Whisk in the egg and egg yolk until combined, then whisk in the vanilla extract. Pour into dry ingredients and mix with a spoon or spatula. Fold in the chocolate chips.

Cover the dough tightly and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or up to 3 days. Chilling overnight prevents overspreading.

## Bake.

Preheat oven to 325°F (163°C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper or silicone mats. If dough chilled longer than 2 hours, let sit at room temperature for about 15 minutes.

Scoop about 3 tablespoons of dough per XL cookie or 2 tablespoons per medium-large cookie. Roll into a ball, then shape into a cylinder. Arrange 3 inches apart on prepared baking sheets.

Bake 13-14 minutes until edges are lightly browned. Centers will look soft. Cool on baking sheet for 10 minutes before transferring to cooling rack.

---

Recipe yields 16 XL cookies or 20 medium/large cookies. Store tightly covered at room temperature for up to 1 week. Light or dark brown sugar both work. Chocolate chunks can be substituted for chocolate chips.
