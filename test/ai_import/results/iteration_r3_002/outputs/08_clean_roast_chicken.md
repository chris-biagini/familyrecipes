# Roast Chicken

Garlic-herb-lemon butter roasted chicken.

Serves: 5
Category: Mains

## Make the butter.

- Butter (unsalted), 100 g: Melted.
- Garlic, 3 cloves: Minced.
- Sage, 1 tbsp: Finely chopped.
- Rosemary, 2 tsp: Finely chopped.
- Parsley, 1 tbsp: Finely chopped.
- Salt, 1/2 tsp
- Black pepper, 1/2 tsp

Mix butter ingredients together, adding juice from 2 lemon wedges.

## Roast the chicken.

- Chicken (whole), 1.75-2 kg: Patted dry.
- Salt
- Black pepper
- Olive oil, 2 tsp
- Lemon, 1: Quartered.
- Rosemary, 3 sprigs
- Dry white wine, 1 cup
- Onion, 1: Quartered.
- Garlic bulb, 1: Halved horizontally.

Remove chicken from fridge 30 minutes before cooking. Preheat oven to 220°C/450°F (standard) or 200°C/430°F (fan/convection), placing shelf in middle.

Place chicken in roasting pan and loosen skin using a spoon on the breast and drumsticks. Prop chicken upright and drizzle herb butter under skin, reserving some for the surface. Drizzle remaining butter over the chicken and squeeze lemon juice over it. Stuff used lemon wedges and rosemary inside the chicken cavity. Tie drumstick ends with string and tuck wing tips underneath. Sprinkle salt and pepper over entire surface.

Place onion and garlic in pan, set chicken on top, pour wine around it, and drizzle with oil.

Roast for 10 minutes at high temperature, then reduce to 180°C/350°F for 1 hour 15 minutes, basting twice (at 30 min and 1 hr). Check that internal temperature reaches 75°C/165°F.

Rest for 15 minutes without covering (to maintain crispy skin). Serve with pan juices; discard onion but use roasted garlic.

---

Substitute low sodium chicken broth for the dry white wine. Imperial equivalents: butter 1 stick, wine 250 ml.
