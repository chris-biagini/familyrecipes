# Roast Chicken

Crispy skin, herb butter under and over.

Serves: 5
Category: Mains
Tags: roasted

## Make the butter.

- Butter (unsalted), 100 g: Melted.
- Garlic, 3 cloves: Minced.
- Sage, 1 tbsp: Finely chopped.
- Rosemary, 2 tsp: Finely chopped.
- Parsley, 1 tbsp: Finely chopped.
- Salt, 1/2 tsp
- Black pepper, 1/2 tsp
- Lemon, 1: Quartered.

Mix butter, garlic, herbs, salt, and pepper. Squeeze juice from 2 lemon wedges into butter; reserve the wedges.

## Prep and roast.

- Chicken (whole), 1.75-2 kg: Patted dry.
- Onion, 1: Quartered.
- Garlic (bulb), 1: Halved horizontally.
- White wine (dry), 1 cup
- Olive oil, 2 tsp
- Rosemary, 3 sprigs
- Salt
- Black pepper

Remove chicken from fridge 30 minutes before cooking. Preheat oven to 220°C/450°F (200°C/430°F convection), rack in middle.

Loosen skin over breast and drumsticks with a spoon. Prop chicken upright and work herb butter under skin; drizzle remainder over the surface. Squeeze remaining lemon wedges over chicken. Stuff used lemon wedges and rosemary sprigs into cavity. Season exterior with salt and pepper. Tie drumstick ends; tuck wing tips under.

Place onion and garlic bulb in roasting pan. Set chicken on top, pour wine around it, drizzle with olive oil.

Roast 10 minutes, then reduce to 180°C/350°F and roast 1 hour 15 minutes, basting at 30 minutes and 1 hour. Internal temp should reach 75°C/165°F.

Rest 15 minutes uncovered to keep skin crispy. Serve with pan juices.

---

Discard the onion after roasting; the roasted garlic is worth serving alongside. Substitute low-sodium chicken broth for the white wine.
