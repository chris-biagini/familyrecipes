# Chewy Chocolate Chip Cookies

Thick, chewy, worth the wait.

Makes: 16 cookies
Category: Baking
Tags: vegetarian, baked

## Mix dough.

- Flour (all-purpose), 2 1/4 cups: Spooned and leveled.
- Baking soda, 1 tsp
- Cornstarch, 1 1/2 tsp
- Salt, 1/2 tsp
- Butter (unsalted), 3/4 cup: Melted and cooled 5 minutes.
- Sugar (brown), 3/4 cup: Packed.
- Sugar (white), 1/2 cup
- Egg, 1
- Egg yolk, 1: Room temperature.
- Vanilla extract, 2 tsp
- Chocolate chips (semi-sweet), 1 1/4 cups

Whisk flour, baking soda, cornstarch, and salt together in large bowl.

In separate bowl, whisk melted butter with both sugars until no lumps remain. Whisk in egg, yolk, and vanilla. Pour into dry ingredients and mix until combined. Fold in chocolate chips.

Cover tightly and refrigerate at least 2 hours, up to 3 days.

## Shape and bake.

Preheat oven to 325°F. Line baking sheets with parchment. If dough chilled longer than 2 hours, rest at room temperature about 15 minutes.

Scoop about 3 tbsp dough per cookie, roll into ball, then shape into cylinder. Space 3 inches apart on prepared sheets.

Bake 13-14 minutes until edges are lightly browned — centers will look underdone. Cool on pan 10 minutes before transferring to rack.

---

Makes 16 XL cookies (3 tbsp each) or 20 medium-large cookies (2 tbsp each). Overnight chilling prevents overspreading. Store tightly covered at room temperature up to 1 week. Dark or light brown sugar both work.
