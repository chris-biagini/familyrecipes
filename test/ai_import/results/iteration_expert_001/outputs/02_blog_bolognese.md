# Spaghetti Bolognese

Quick weeknight ragu with real depth.

Serves: 5
Category: Mains
Tags: weeknight, italian

## Make the sauce.

- Olive oil, 1 1/2 tbsp
- Onion, 1: Finely chopped.
- Garlic, 2 cloves: Minced.
- Beef mince, 500 g
- Red wine (dry), 125 mL
- Beef bouillon cubes, 2: Crumbled.
- Tomatoes (canned, crushed), 800 g
- Tomato paste, 2 tbsp
- Sugar (white), 2 tsp
- Worcestershire sauce, 2 tsp
- Bay leaves (dried), 2
- Thyme (fresh), 2 sprigs
- Salt, 3/4 tsp
- Black pepper, 1/2 tsp

Heat oil in large pot over medium-high. Sauté onion and garlic about 3 minutes until softened and light golden.

Raise heat to high, add beef, and cook breaking up until browned. Add wine, scraping the bottom, and simmer 1 minute until alcohol smell is gone.

Add remaining sauce ingredients. Bring to simmer, reduce to medium, and cook uncovered 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add water if sauce thickens too much. Taste and adjust salt.

## Cook pasta and serve.

- Spaghetti (dried), 400 g
- Parmesan: Optional.
- Parsley: Optional.

Cook spaghetti in well-salted boiling water until 1 minute shy of al dente. Reserve a mug of pasta water before draining.

Add drained pasta to sauce with about 125 mL pasta water over medium heat. Toss 1 1/2-2 minutes until pasta is red and sauce clings. Divide into bowls and garnish with parmesan and parsley.

---

Substitute half the beef with pork mince. Red wine can be replaced with water or beef stock. Substitute fresh thyme with 1/2 tsp dried thyme or oregano. Crushed tomatoes can be replaced with passata. Add sugar only if needed to balance acidity. Parmesan and parsley are garnish.

**Slow simmer option:** After adding sauce ingredients, add 3/4 cup water, cover, and simmer on very low 2-2 1/2 hours, stirring every 30 minutes. Uncover and simmer 20 minutes to thicken.
