# Roast Chicken

Garlic-herb-lemon butter roasted chicken with pan juices.

Serves: 5

## Prepare the chicken.

- Chicken (whole), 1.75-2 kg
- Lemon, 1: Quartered.
- Rosemary sprigs, 3

Remove chicken from fridge 30 minutes before cooking. Preheat oven to 220°C (standard) or 200°C (fan/convection), placing shelf in middle.

Place chicken in roasting pan and loosen skin using a spoon on the breast and drumsticks. Stuff lemon wedges and rosemary inside the chicken cavity. Tie drumstick ends with string and tuck wing tips underneath.

## Make the herb butter and season.

- Butter (unsalted), 100 g: Melted.
- Garlic, 3 cloves: Minced.
- Sage, 1 tbsp: Finely chopped.
- Rosemary, 2 tsp: Finely chopped.
- Parsley, 1 tbsp: Finely chopped.
- Salt
- Black pepper
- Olive oil, 2 tsp

Mix butter, garlic, sage, rosemary, parsley, salt, and pepper together, adding juice from 2 lemon wedges.

Prop chicken upright and drizzle herb butter under skin, reserving some for the surface. Drizzle remaining butter over the chicken and squeeze lemon juice over it. Sprinkle salt and pepper over entire surface.

## Roast the chicken.

- Dry white wine, 1 cup
- Onion, 1: Quartered.
- Garlic bulb, 1: Halved horizontally.

Place onion and garlic in pan, set chicken on top, and pour wine around it. Drizzle with olive oil.

Roast for 10 minutes at high temperature, then reduce to 180°C for 1 hour 15 minutes, basting twice (at 30 minutes and 1 hour). Check that internal temperature reaches 75°C.

Rest for 15 minutes without covering (to maintain crispy skin).

Serve with pan juices; discard onion but use roasted garlic.
