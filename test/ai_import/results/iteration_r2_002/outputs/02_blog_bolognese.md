# Spaghetti Bolognese

Quick 30-minute Bolognese with depth of flavor.

Serves: 5

## Make the sauce.

- Olive oil, 1 1/2 tbsp
- Onion, 1: Finely chopped.
- Garlic, 2 cloves: Minced.
- Beef mince (ground beef), 1 lb

Heat oil in a large pot or deep skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion and garlic; cook 3 minutes until light golden and softened.

Turn heat to high and add beef. Cook, breaking it up as you go, until browned.

- Red wine (dry), 1/2 cup

Add red wine. Bring to simmer and cook 1 minute, scraping the bottom of the pot, until the alcohol smell is gone.

- Beef bouillon cubes, 2: Crumbled or granulated.
- Tomatoes (canned, crushed), 800 g
- Tomato paste, 2 tbsp
- Sugar (white), 2 tsp
- Worcestershire sauce, 2 tsp
- Bay leaves (dried), 2
- Thyme (fresh), 2 sprigs
- Salt (kosher), 3/4 tsp
- Black pepper, 1/2 tsp

Add remaining ingredients. Stir, bring to a simmer then reduce heat to medium so it bubbles gently. Cook 20-30 minutes (no lid), adding water if sauce gets too thick. Stir occasionally.

Taste and add more salt if desired.

## Cook the pasta.

- Spaghetti (dried), 400 g

Bring large pot of salted water to boil. Add pasta and cook per packet directions minus 1 minute.

Scoop out a mug of pasta cooking water and set aside, then drain pasta.

Add pasta into bolognese sauce with about 1/2 cup reserved pasta water over medium heat. Toss gently 1 1/2-2 minutes until spaghetti turns red and sauce thickens.

Divide between bowls. Garnish with parmesan and parsley if desired.

---

Recipe by Nagi from RecipeTin Eats. Substitute water or beef broth/stock for the red wine. Or use tomato passata instead of canned crushed tomato. Half pork, half beef may be substituted for the all-beef mince. Dried thyme or oregano may be substituted for fresh thyme. Parmesan cheese and finely chopped parsley are optional garnishes. For a slow simmer option, add 3/4 cup water, cover with lid and simmer on very low for 2-2.5 hours, stirring every 30 minutes. Uncover and simmer 20 minutes to thicken sauce.
