# Beef Wellington

Worth the effort.

Serves: 6
Category: Mains
Tags: holiday

## Sear and coat beef.

- Beef tenderloin (center-cut), about 2 lbs: Trimmed of silverskin and fat.
- Salt
- Black pepper
- Vegetable oil, 2 tbsp
- Horseradish (prepared), 2 tbsp
- Mustard (Dijon), 1 tbsp

Tie tenderloin at 1-inch intervals with butcher's twine. Season liberally with salt and pepper.

Heat oil in a cast iron or stainless steel skillet over high heat until smoking. Sear tenderloin without moving until well browned on first side, about 2 minutes, then rotate and brown all sides, about 10 minutes total. Transfer to a plate.

Combine horseradish, mustard, and 1 tsp black pepper. When cool enough to handle, remove twine and rub mixture all over tenderloin. Refrigerate uncovered.

## Make duxelles; sear foie gras.

- Mushrooms, 1 lb: Roughly chopped.
- Butter (unsalted), 4 tbsp
- Shallots, 2: Finely diced.
- Thyme (fresh), 2 tsp: Minced.
- Cognac, 1/2 cup
- Heavy cream, 1/2 cup
- Soy sauce, 2 tsp
- Foie gras (fresh), 4 oz
- Vegetable oil, 1/2 tsp

Pulse mushrooms in a food processor in two batches until very finely chopped, about 10 short pulses per batch.

Heat butter in a 12-inch skillet over medium-high heat. Cook mushrooms, stirring occasionally, until they've released their liquid and begin to sizzle, about 8 minutes, then continue until browned with bits sticking to pan, about 4 minutes more. Add shallots and thyme; cook until softened, about 2 minutes.

Add Cognac and scrape up browned bits. Cook until nearly dry, about 4 minutes. Add cream and soy sauce; cook, stirring frequently, until thick and collecting in one mass. Season with salt and pepper. Transfer to a bowl and refrigerate.

Season foie gras with salt and pepper. Heat 1/2 tsp oil in a small skillet over high heat until smoking. Sear foie gras without moving until well browned, about 30 seconds per side. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate. Pour rendered fat into mushroom mixture and stir to combine. Slice each piece of foie gras in half horizontally. Refrigerate everything for at least 30 minutes.

## Assemble inner roll; chill.

- Phyllo dough, 1 sheet
- Prosciutto, 1/4 lb: Sliced paper thin.

Lay a double layer of plastic wrap (about 2 feet × 1 foot) on a cutting board. Lay phyllo on top. Shingle prosciutto over phyllo in an even, overlapping layer, leaving a 2-inch border along top and bottom edges. Spread duxelles evenly over prosciutto.

Place tenderloin along the bottom edge. Lay sliced foie gras evenly over top. Using the plastic wrap to tighten as you go, roll tenderloin in the mushroom/ham/phyllo layer. Re-wrap tightly in fresh plastic wrap, twisting ends to secure. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes.

## Wrap in puff pastry; chill.

- Flour: For dusting.
- Puff pastry, 14 oz: Thawed.
- Egg, 1: Beaten.

On a lightly floured surface, roll puff pastry into a rectangle at least 4 inches wider than the beef roll on its shorter side.

Unwrap beef roll and place along the bottom edge of the pastry, foie gras side up. Brush 6 inches of pastry just above the roll with beaten egg. Roll beef in pastry until completely wrapped — seam on bottom, foie gras side up. Trim excess.

To seal the ends: fold side flaps in toward center, fold top flaps down, trim bottom flaps. Flip roll over, fold remaining flaps up to seal completely, then flip right-side up. Transfer to a plate and refrigerate at least 30 minutes.

## Bake and serve.

- Coarse sea salt
- Chives, 1 bunch: Minced.

Preheat oven to 425°F, rack in center. Transfer Wellington to a foil-lined baking sheet. Brush all over with remaining beaten egg, score a decorative pattern with a paring knife, and sprinkle liberally with coarse sea salt. Bake until pastry is golden and center registers 110°F for rare or 120°F for medium-rare, 35-45 minutes. Rest 10 minutes.

Loosen from foil with a thin spatula and transfer to a carving board. Slice off ends and carve. Sprinkle cut surfaces with chives and more coarse salt.

---

Center-cut beef tenderloin is also known as Châteaubriand. Substitute spicy brown or hot English mustard for the Dijon. Use button, cremini, shiitake, portabello, or a mix of mushrooms. Cognac can be replaced with any brandy or barrel-aged spirit such as bourbon. Foie gras pâté can substitute for fresh foie gras — skip the foie gras searing and pour no fat into the duxelles; instead, spread pâté directly over the tenderloin during assembly. Use a high-quality all-butter puff pastry such as Dufour, or make your own.
