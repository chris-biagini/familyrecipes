# Cubano

From the food truck scene.

Serves: 6
Category: Mains

## Advance prep: roast pork.

- Orange juice, 3 tbsp
- Lime juice, 3 tbsp
- Garlic, 4 tsp: Minced.
- Mirin, 2 tsp
- Cumin, 1 tsp
- Oregano (dried), 1 tsp
- Coriander, 1 tsp
- Salt, 1/2 tsp
- Black pepper, 1/2 tsp
- Pork loin (boneless), 1 lb

Combine marinade ingredients in a gallon zip-top bag. Add pork, seal, and turn to coat. Marinate refrigerated at least 2 hours, up to 24. Remove from marinade, place on a rack in a roasting pan. Roast at 350°F (175°C) until internal temp reaches 145°F (62°C), 1-1 1/2 hours. Tent with foil and rest 15 minutes. Cover tightly and chill overnight — cold pork slices much more cleanly. Slice very thinly before using.

## Cook meat; press sandwiches.

- Ham (deli smoked), 18 slices (18 oz)
- Cuban bread, 3 loaves (8 oz each): Split.
- Swiss cheese, 12 slices
- Dill pickle, 12 slices: Sliced thin, lengthwise.
- Yellow mustard, 1/2 cup
- Butter, 12 tbsp: Melted.

Heat cast-iron skillet or griddle over medium-high until very hot. Working in batches, cook roasted pork and ham, turning once, until warmed and slightly crispy, about 2 minutes. Keep warm.

Return skillet to heat. Toast bread cut-side down, about 1 minute per batch. Divide pork and ham among bottom halves; top each with 2 Swiss slices and 2 pickle slices. Generously spread mustard on tops; close sandwiches mustard-side down.

Brush skillet with 2 tbsp butter. Add 2 sandwiches; brush tops with 2 more tbsp butter. Weight down with a heavy skillet. Cook 6-8 minutes, turning halfway, until bread is golden and cheese is melted. Repeat in batches of 2 with remaining sandwiches and butter.

---

Based on a recipe from Jaelyn Luong (Allrecipes).

Substitute pulled pork for roasted pork; skip the advance prep and use 1 1/2 cups (12 oz).
