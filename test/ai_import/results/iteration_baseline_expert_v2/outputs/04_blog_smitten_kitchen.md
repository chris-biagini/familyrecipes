# Caramelized Onion and Gruyère Biscuits

Big, cheesy, deeply savory.

Makes: 10 biscuits
Category: Baking
Tags: vegetarian, baked

## Caramelize onions.

- Butter (unsalted), 1 tbsp
- Olive oil, 1 tbsp
- Onions, 2 small: Halved and thinly sliced.

Melt butter with olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onions, reduce to low, cover, and steam about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove lid and cook until deep brown, 10-20 more minutes. Set aside to cool.

## Make dough; shape and bake.

- Flour (all-purpose), 2 3/4 cups (345 g)
- Sugar (white), 1 tbsp (15 g)
- Baking powder, 2 tsp
- Baking soda, 1 tsp
- Salt (coarse), 3/4 tsp
- Butter (unsalted), 8 tbsp: Cold, cut into 1/2-inch cubes.
- Gruyère, 4 oz (115 g): Cut into 1/2-inch cubes.
- Buttermilk, 1 cup
- Flaky sea salt
- Black pepper

Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment.

Combine flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Cut in cold butter until crumbly with pea-sized pieces — by hand or in a food processor. Stir in cheese. Pour buttermilk over cooled onions, stir to combine, then add to flour mixture and stir together. Knead a few times in the bowl if dry; a few floury spots are fine.

Turn out onto a floured surface and roll to 1-inch thickness. Stamp out with a floured 3-inch cutter; space apart on prepared baking sheet. Re-roll scraps as needed. Sprinkle with flaky salt and pepper. Bake 20-23 minutes until deep golden and cheese is bubbly at the edges.

---

Best on the first day; reheat on day two. Unbaked biscuits can be frozen; thaw before baking. Kosher salt works in place of coarse. Substitute another Swiss-style cheese for the Gruyère. Make your own buttermilk if needed.

Based on a recipe from Alyce Shields at Pronto by Bar Bambino, via Tasting Table.
