# Good Eats Beef Stew

Low and slow, worth every hour.

Serves: 4
Category: Mains
Tags: comfort-food, american

## Sear and braise ribs.

- Tomato paste, 1/4 cup
- Apple cider vinegar, 1/4 cup
- Worcestershire sauce, 1 1/2 tsp
- Paprika, 1 tbsp
- Dried herbs, 1 1/2 tsp
- Short ribs (English-cut), 3 lbs
- Kosher salt, 1 tbsp

Whisk together tomato paste, vinegar, Worcestershire, paprika, and dried herbs. Set aside.

Season ribs with salt. Sear on a hot griddle over medium-high until browned on all sides. Toss in paste mixture to coat. Seal tightly in heavy-duty foil, set package in a metal pan, and place in a cold oven on the middle rack. Set to 250°F and cook 4 hours.

## Degrease braising liquid.

Remove from oven. Poke a hole in the foil pack over a heatproof container and drain the liquid. Refrigerate until fat separates, about 1 hour, then transfer to the freezer until the fat cap solidifies, about 1 more hour. Let ribs rest at room temperature while the liquid cools.

Remove the solidified fat cap; measure out 1 tbsp and set aside. Discard or save the rest.

## Cook vegetables; finish and serve.

- Kosher salt, 1 tsp
- Onion, 1 large: Thinly sliced.
- Red potatoes, 1 lb: Unpeeled, sliced.
- Black pepper, a pinch
- Parsley, 1 tbsp: Chopped.

Melt the reserved 1 tbsp fat in a large saucier over medium heat. Add onion and salt; stir to separate into rings. Cook 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add potatoes and black pepper, then pour in the defatted braising liquid. Cover tightly, reduce to low, and cook until potatoes are fork-tender, about 30 minutes.

Meanwhile, remove meat from bones, discarding connective tissue. Once potatoes are tender, uncover and set meat atop vegetables. Cover and cook 10 more minutes. Serve sprinkled with parsley.

---

Dried herbs can be any combination of thyme, oregano, and rosemary. Based on a recipe from Good Eats (Alton Brown).
