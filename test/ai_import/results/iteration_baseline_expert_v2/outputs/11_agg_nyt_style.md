# No-Knead Bread

Worth the wait.

Makes: 1 loaf
Category: Bread
Tags: make-ahead

## Mix dough; ferment.

- Flour (all-purpose), 3 1/3 cups
- Yeast (instant), generous 1/4 tsp
- Salt (kosher), 2 tsp
- Water, 1 1/2 cups

Combine flour, yeast, and salt in a large bowl. Add water; stir until blended — dough will be shaggy and sticky. Cover with plastic wrap. Rest at room temperature (about 70°F) for at least 12 hours, preferably 18. Dough is ready when surface is dotted with bubbles.

## Shape dough; proof.

- Cornmeal

Lightly flour a work surface; turn out dough, sprinkle with a little more flour, and fold over once or twice. Cover loosely; rest 15 minutes.

Generously coat a cotton towel (not terry cloth) with flour, cornmeal, or wheat bran. Shape dough into a ball and place seam-side down on towel; dust top with more cornmeal. Cover with a second cotton towel; let rise about 2 hours until more than doubled and dough doesn't spring back when poked.

## Bake.

At least 30 minutes before dough is ready, heat oven to 450°F with a 6-8 qt heavy covered pot (cast iron, enamel, Pyrex, or ceramic) inside. Carefully remove hot pot; turn dough in seam-side up — it may look uneven, but it straightens as it bakes. Cover and bake 30 minutes; remove lid and bake another 15-30 minutes until deeply browned. Cool on rack.

---

Based on a recipe by Jim Lahey, adapted by Mark Bittman for The New York Times. Bread flour can be substituted for all-purpose flour. Wheat bran can be substituted for cornmeal.
