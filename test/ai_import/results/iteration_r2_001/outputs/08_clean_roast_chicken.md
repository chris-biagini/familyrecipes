# Roast Chicken

Slathered with a garlic-herb-lemon butter then oven roasted to golden crispy perfection. Juicy on the inside with liquid gold pan juices loaded with flavour.

Serves: 5

## Prepare the herb butter.

- Butter (unsalted), 100 g: Softened to room temperature.
- Garlic, 4 cloves: Minced.
- Lemon, 1: Zest only.
- Rosemary (fresh), 1 1/2 tbsp: Finely chopped.
- Parsley (fresh), 1 1/2 tbsp: Finely chopped.
- Sage (fresh), 1 tbsp: Finely chopped.
- Salt
- Black pepper

Mix together softened butter, minced garlic, lemon zest, rosemary, parsley, sage, salt, and pepper until well combined.

## Roast the chicken.

- Chicken (whole), 1.75-2 kg
- Olive oil, 2 tbsp
- Onion, 1: Halved.
- Garlic bulb, 1: Halved.
- Lemon, 1: Halved.
- Dry white wine or chicken broth, 1 cup

Pat the chicken dry. Loosen the skin on the chicken by sliding your fingers under the skin on the breasts and thighs without tearing it. Spread the herb butter mixture under the skin, distributing it evenly over the breasts and thighs. Rub the outside of the chicken with olive oil, salt, and pepper.

Place the halved onion, garlic bulb, and lemon inside the cavity of the chicken. Place the chicken breast-side up in a roasting pan. Pour the wine or broth around the chicken (not over it).

Roast at 220°C for 20 minutes. Reduce heat to 180°C and continue roasting for 50-60 minutes, until the internal temperature reaches 75°C when measured in the thickest part of the thigh without touching bone. Baste the chicken with pan juices halfway through cooking.

Remove from oven and let rest for 10 minutes before carving.
