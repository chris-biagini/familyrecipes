# Beef Wellington

Serves: 6
Category: Mains
Tags: holiday

## Sear and prep beef.

- Beef tenderloin (center-cut), about 2 lbs: Trimmed of silverskin and fat.
- Salt
- Black pepper
- Vegetable oil, 2 tbsp
- Horseradish (prepared), 2 tbsp
- Mustard (Dijon), 1 tbsp

Tie tenderloin at 1-inch intervals with butcher's twine; trim ends. Season liberally with salt and pepper.

Heat oil in a cast iron or stainless steel skillet over high heat until smoking. Sear without moving until well-browned, about 2 minutes. Rotate and continue until browned on all sides, about 10 minutes total. Transfer to a plate.

Mix horseradish, mustard, and 1 tsp black pepper. Once cool, remove twine and rub mixture over all surfaces. Refrigerate uncovered.

## Make duxelles; sear foie gras.

- Mushrooms, 1 lb: Cleaned, trimmed, and roughly chopped.
- Butter (unsalted), 4 tbsp
- Shallots, 2: Finely diced.
- Thyme, 2 tsp: Finely minced.
- Cognac, 1/2 cup
- Heavy cream, 1/2 cup
- Soy sauce, 2 tsp
- Foie gras (fresh), 4 oz
- Vegetable oil, 1/2 tsp

Pulse mushrooms in a food processor in two batches until very finely chopped, about 10 short pulses per batch.

Melt butter in a 12-inch skillet over medium-high. Cook mushrooms, stirring occasionally, until liquid evaporates and they begin to sizzle, about 8 minutes. Continue until browned and leaving bits stuck to the pan, about 4 more minutes. Add shallots and thyme; cook until softened, about 2 minutes.

Add cognac, scraping up browned bits. Cook until nearly dry, about 4 minutes. Add cream and soy sauce; cook, stirring frequently, until thick and the mixture collects in one mass when you shake the pan. Season with salt and pepper. Transfer to a bowl and refrigerate.

Season foie gras with salt and pepper. Heat oil in a small cast iron or stainless steel skillet over high heat until smoking. Sear without moving until well-browned, about 30 seconds per side. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate. Pour rendered fat into mushroom mixture and stir to combine. Slice each piece of foie gras in half horizontally. Refrigerate everything at least 30 minutes.

## Roll in prosciutto and phyllo.

- Phyllo dough, 1 sheet
- Prosciutto, 1/4 lb: Sliced paper thin.

Lay a double layer of plastic wrap about 2 feet long and 1 foot wide on a cutting board. Lay phyllo on top. Shingle prosciutto in an overlapping layer over the phyllo, leaving a 2-inch border along top and bottom edges. Spread mushroom mixture evenly over the ham.

Place tenderloin along the bottom edge. Lay sliced foie gras evenly over the top. Using the plastic wrap to help tighten, roll beef up in the mushroom, ham, and phyllo. Re-wrap tightly with fresh plastic wrap, twisting the ends to seal. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes.

## Wrap in puff pastry.

- Flour
- Puff pastry, 14 oz: Thawed.
- Egg, 1: Beaten.

Dust a board lightly with flour. Roll puff pastry into a rectangle at least 4 inches wider on the short side than the beef roll.

Place beef roll along the bottom edge, foie gras side facing up. Brush the 6 inches of pastry just above the roll with egg. Roll beef in pastry until completely wrapped — seam at the bottom, foie gras side facing up. Trim excess. Fold in the side flaps, then fold the top flaps down, and trim the bottom flaps. Flip the roll, fold up end flaps to seal, then flip back right-side up. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes.

## Bake and serve.

- Sea salt (coarse)
- Chives, 1 bunch: Finely minced.

Preheat oven to 425°F with rack in center. Transfer Wellington to a foil-lined baking sheet. Brush all over with remaining egg. Score a decorative pattern in the pastry with a paring knife. Sprinkle liberally with coarse salt.

Bake until pastry is golden and center registers 110°F for rare or 120°F for medium-rare, 35-45 minutes. Rest 10 minutes.

Loosen from foil with a thin spatula, transfer to a carving board, and slice off the ends. Carve, sprinkle cut surfaces with chives and more coarse salt, and serve.

---

Center-cut beef tenderloin is also known as Châteaubriand. Substitute spicy brown or hot English mustard for the Dijon. Substitute other brandy or bourbon for the Cognac. Mushrooms can be button, cremini, shiitake, portobello, or a mix. Use high-quality all-butter puff pastry; Dufour is recommended. Foie gras pâté can substitute for fresh foie gras — skip the searing step and instead spread the pâté directly over the tenderloin during assembly.
