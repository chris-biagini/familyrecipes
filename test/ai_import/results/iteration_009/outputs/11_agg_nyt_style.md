# No-Knead Bread

No effort, just time.

Makes: 1 loaf (1 1/2 lbs)
Category: Bread

## Mix dough; ferment.

- Flour (all-purpose), 3 1/3 cups
- Yeast (instant), generous 1/4 tsp
- Salt (kosher), 2 tsp
- Water, 1 1/2 cups

Combine flour, yeast, and salt in a large bowl. Add water and stir until blended — dough will be shaggy and sticky. Cover and rest at about 70°F for at least 12 hours, preferably 18, until surface is dotted with bubbles.

## Shape dough; proof.

- Cornmeal

Flour a work surface, place dough, sprinkle with a little more flour, fold once or twice. Cover loosely and rest 15 minutes. Using minimal flour, quickly shape dough into a ball. Generously coat a cotton towel (not terry cloth) with flour or cornmeal; place dough seam side down on towel and dust top. Cover with another cotton towel and let rise about 2 hours, until more than doubled and dough doesn't spring back when poked.

## Bake.

At least 30 minutes before dough is ready, heat oven to 450°F with a 6-8 qt heavy covered pot (cast iron, enamel, Pyrex, or ceramic) inside. Remove pot carefully. Turn dough into pot seam side up — it may look like a mess but will straighten as it bakes. Shake once or twice if uneven. Cover and bake 30 minutes, then remove lid and bake another 15-30 minutes until beautifully browned. Cool on a rack.

---

Bread flour may be substituted for all-purpose. Wheat bran may be substituted for cornmeal.

Based on a recipe from Jim Lahey, adapted by Mark Bittman for The New York Times.
