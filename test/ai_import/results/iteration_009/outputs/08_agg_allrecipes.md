# Cubano

The food-truck Cubano from *Chef*.

Serves: 6
Category: Mains

## Advance prep: marinate and roast pork.

- Pork loin roast (boneless), 1 lb
- Orange juice, 3 tbsp
- Lime juice, 3 tbsp
- Garlic, 4 tsp: Minced.
- Mirin, 2 tsp
- Cumin, 1 tsp
- Oregano (dried), 1 tsp
- Coriander, 1 tsp
- Salt, 1/2 tsp
- Black pepper, 1/2 tsp

Combine juices, garlic, mirin, cumin, oregano, coriander, salt, and pepper in a gallon zip-top bag. Add pork; seal and turn to coat. Marinate in refrigerator at least 2 hours or up to 24.

Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Place pork on a rack in a roasting pan. Roast 1-1 1/2 hours until an instant-read thermometer registers 145°F (62°C). Tent with foil and rest 15 minutes. Chill overnight; slice very thin when ready to use. Yields about 12 oz (1 1/2 cups) sliced.

## Cook meats; toast bread.

- Ham (deli smoked), 18 slices (18 oz)
- Cuban bread, 3 loaves (8 oz each): Split lengthwise.

Heat a cast-iron skillet or griddle over medium-high heat until very hot. To test: sprinkle water on surface — it should sizzle and evaporate immediately. Working in batches, cook pork and ham, turning once, until warmed and slightly crispy, about 2 minutes. Keep warm.

Cut each loaf crosswise into halves. Toast cut-side down in same skillet, about 1 minute per batch.

## Assemble and press.

- Swiss cheese, 12 slices
- Dill pickle, 12 slices: Very thin, lengthwise.
- Yellow mustard, 1/2 cup
- Butter, 12 tbsp: Melted.

Divide pork and ham among bottom bread halves; top each with 2 cheese slices and 2 pickle slices. Spread tops generously with mustard; close sandwiches.

Brush skillet with 2 tbsp butter. Add 2 sandwiches; brush tops with 2 tbsp butter. Weight down with a heavy skillet. Toast, turning once, until bread is golden and cheese is melted, 6-8 minutes. Repeat with remaining sandwiches and butter.

---

Substitute pulled pork for the roasted pork (use 1 1/2 cups / 12 oz).

Based on a recipe from Jaelyn Luong of Allrecipes.
