# Caramelized Onion and Gruyère Biscuits

Makes: 10 biscuits
Category: Baking

## Caramelize the onions.

- Butter (unsalted), 1 tbsp
- Olive oil, 1 tbsp
- Onions, 2 small: Halved and thinly sliced.

Melt butter with olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onions, reduce heat to low, cover and steam about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove lid; cook, stirring occasionally, until deep brown, 10-20 more minutes (up to 10 additional if needed). Cool completely.

## Make dough; cut and bake.

- Flour (all-purpose), 2 3/4 cups (345 g)
- Sugar (white), 1 tbsp (15 g)
- Baking powder, 2 tsp
- Baking soda, 1 tsp
- Salt (coarse), 3/4 tsp
- Butter (unsalted), 8 tbsp: Cold, diced into 1/2-inch pieces.
- Gruyère, 4 oz (115 g): Cut into 1/2-inch cubes.
- Buttermilk, 1 cup
- Flaky sea salt
- Black pepper

Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment.

In a bowl or food processor, combine flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Cut in butter until crumbly with pieces no larger than a small pea — use fingertips, a pastry blender, or food processor pulses. Transfer to bowl if using a processor. Stir in cheese. Stir buttermilk into cooled onions, then add to flour mixture; stir to combine, using hands to bring it together if needed — a few floury spots are fine.

Turn out onto a floured surface, roll to 1-inch thickness, and stamp out 3-inch rounds. Re-roll scraps. Space on baking sheet, sprinkle with flaky salt and pepper, and bake until deep golden-brown with cheese melted and bubbly at the edges, 20-23 minutes.

---

Kosher salt works in place of coarse salt. Another Swiss-style cheese works in place of gruyère. Buttermilk can be made at home. Best warm on day one; reheat on day two.

Based on a recipe from Alyce Shields at Pronto by Bar Bambino, via Tasting Table.
