# Popover-Topped Pot Pie

Billowing popover lid over a simple vegetarian filling.

Serves: 4
Category: Mains
Tags: vegetarian

## Make the filling.

- Olive oil (extra-virgin), 1/4 cup plus 1 tbsp
- Potatoes (Yukon Gold), 12 oz: Peeled, cut into 1/2" pieces.
- Asparagus, 1 bunch: Trimmed, cut into 1/2" pieces.
- Carrots, 2: Peeled, halved lengthwise, cut into 1/4"-thick half-moons.
- Celery, 2 stalks: Thinly sliced.
- Shallots, 2: Finely chopped.
- Peas (frozen), 1 cup
- Garlic, 6 cloves: Finely grated.
- Flour (all-purpose), 1/4 cup (31 g)
- Vegetable broth (low-sodium), 2 cups
- White wine (dry), 1/2 cup
- Heavy cream, 2/3 cup
- Dijon mustard, 1 tbsp
- Lemon zest, 1 1/2 tsp: Finely grated.
- Dill, 1/3 cup: Finely chopped.
- Kosher salt, 1 1/2 tsp
- Black pepper, 3/4 tsp

Heat oil in a large (14-cup+) high-sided ovenproof skillet or Dutch oven over medium. Add potatoes; cook, stirring often, 2 minutes. Add asparagus, carrots, celery, shallots, and peas; cook, stirring often, until potatoes are not quite fork-tender, 15-18 minutes.

Add garlic and flour; stir until vegetables are coated and no dry flour remains. Gradually pour in broth and wine, stirring constantly, then add cream, mustard, lemon zest, dill, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer; cook until sauce coats a spoon, about 1 minute. Remove from heat; taste and adjust mustard, salt, and pepper. Let sit undisturbed 20 minutes to 1 hour — do not stir; a slight skin should form on the surface.

## Make the popover batter.

- Eggs, 5
- Parmesan, 1 oz: Finely grated.
- Whole milk, 1 1/3 cups
- Baking powder, 1/2 tsp
- Flour (all-purpose), 1 1/4 cups (156 g)
- Kosher salt, 1 tsp
- Black pepper, 1/4 tsp
- Dill, 2 tbsp: Finely chopped.

While filling rests, preheat oven to 425°F with rack in center.

Blend eggs on high speed until light and fluffy. Add Parmesan, milk, baking powder, flour, salt, and pepper; blend on medium just until smooth. Stir in dill. Cover tightly; let sit until filling is ready.

## Bake.

Holding a spoon upside down over filling, gradually pour batter over it. Bake until topping is deep golden brown and very puffed, 45-55 minutes.

---

Quantities use Diamond Crystal kosher salt; if using Morton, use 1 tsp in the filling and 1/2 tsp in the batter. Add up to 1 additional tbsp Dijon to the filling after cooking, to taste.

Based on a recipe from Jesse Szewczyk.
