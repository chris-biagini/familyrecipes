# Beef-and-Bean Chili

A proper chili calls for lots of beef!

Serves: 8
Category: Mains

- Ground beef, 4 lb
- Garlic, 6 cloves: Minced.
- Tomato sauce, 2 8-oz cans
- Tomato paste, 1 6-oz can
- Chili powder, 1/4 cup: Plus more to taste.
- Cumin (ground), 2 tsp
- Oregano (ground), 2 tsp
- Salt (kosher), 2 tsp: Plus more to taste.
- Cayenne pepper, 1/2 tsp: Plus more to taste.
- Beans (kidney and pinto), 4 15-oz cans: Drained and rinsed.
- Masa harina, 1/2 cup
- Cheddar (shredded)
- Bacon (crumbled)
- Jalapeños (sliced)

In a large heavy pot, brown the ground beef with the garlic until it's totally cooked. Drain off most of the excess fat, leaving a little behind for moisture and flavor.

Add the tomato sauce, tomato paste, chili powder, cumin, oregano, salt and cayenne. Stir together well, cover the pot and reduce the heat to low. Simmer for 1 hour, stirring occasionally. If the liquid level seems low, add up to 1 1/2 cups water to keep the chili from burning.

Stir the beans into the chili. In a small bowl, combine the masa harina with 1/2 cup water and stir with a fork until smooth, then stir into the chili. Let simmer for another 10 minutes to thicken. Taste and adjust the seasonings. Top with cheese, bacon and jalapeños.

---

Prep time: 30 minutes. Total time: 2 hours.

Shredded cheddar, crumbled bacon, and sliced jalapeños are toppings.

Or regular cornmeal in place of masa harina.

Give canned beans a rinse under cold water—they can be slimy right out of the can.

By Ree Drummond.
