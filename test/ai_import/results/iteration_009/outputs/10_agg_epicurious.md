# Good Eats Beef Stew

Serves: 4
Category: Mains

## Make paste; sear and braise.

- Tomato paste, 1/4 cup
- Apple cider vinegar, 1/4 cup
- Worcestershire sauce, 1 1/2 tsp
- Paprika, 1 tbsp
- Dried herbs, 1 1/2 tsp
- Short ribs (English-cut), 3 lbs
- Salt (kosher), 1 tbsp

Whisk together tomato paste, vinegar, Worcestershire sauce, paprika, and dried herbs. Season ribs with salt. Heat griddle over medium-high until a drop of water bounces, then sear until browned on all sides. Toss in paste to coat, wrap tightly in heavy-duty foil, and set in a metal pan. Place in a cold oven on the middle rack, set to 250°F, and cook 4 hours.

Remove from oven. Poke a hole in the foil pack over a heatproof container and drain the liquid. Refrigerate about 1 hour until fat separates, then transfer to the freezer until the fat cap solidifies, about 1 hour more. Let ribs rest at room temperature.

## Cook vegetables.

- Salt (kosher), 1 tsp
- Onion, 1: Thinly sliced.
- Potatoes (red), 1 lb: Unpeeled, sliced.
- Black pepper

Remove fat cap from defatted liquid; reserve 1 tbsp of the fat. Melt fat in a large saucier over medium heat. Add onion and salt, stirring to separate into rings. Cook 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add potatoes and a pinch of black pepper, then the braising liquid. Cover tightly, reduce to low, and cook until potatoes are fork tender, about 30 minutes.

## Finish and serve.

- Parsley, 1 tbsp: Chopped.

While potatoes cook, cut meat from bone, discarding connective tissue. Once potatoes are tender, set meat atop vegetables, cover, and cook 10 minutes more. Serve sprinkled with parsley.

---

Any combination of thyme, oregano, and rosemary for the dried herbs. Reserve the remaining braising fat for another use.
