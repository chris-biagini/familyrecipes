# Cubano

The movie sandwich.

Serves: 6
Category: Mains

## Advance prep: marinate and roast pork.

- Orange juice, 3 tbsp
- Lime juice, 3 tbsp
- Garlic, 4 tsp: Minced.
- Mirin, 2 tsp
- Cumin, 1 tsp
- Oregano (dried), 1 tsp
- Coriander, 1 tsp
- Salt, 1/2 tsp
- Black pepper, 1/2 tsp
- Pork loin roast (boneless), 1 lb

Combine juices, garlic, mirin, and spices in a gallon zip-top bag. Add pork, seal, and turn to coat. Marinate in refrigerator at least 2 hours, up to 24.

Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Remove pork from marinade; place on a rack in a roasting pan. Roast 1-1 1/2 hours until an instant-read thermometer reads 145°F (62°C). Tent with foil; rest 15 minutes. Chill overnight for easier slicing — yields about 12 oz cooked.

## Cook meats; toast bread.

- Roasted pork, 1 1/2 cups (12 oz): Very thinly sliced.
- Ham (smoked), 18 slices (18 oz)
- Cuban bread, 3 loaves (8 oz each): Split.

Heat cast-iron skillet or griddle over medium-high until very hot — a sprinkle of water should sizzle and evaporate immediately. Working in batches, cook pork and ham, turning once, until warmed and slightly crispy, about 2 minutes. Keep warm.

Cut each loaf in half crosswise. Toast cut-side down in hot skillet, about 1 minute per batch.

## Assemble and press sandwiches.

- Swiss cheese, 12 slices
- Dill pickle, 12 slices: Sliced very thin lengthwise.
- Yellow mustard, 1/2 cup
- Butter, 12 tbsp: Melted.

Layer pork and ham on bottom halves. Top each with 2 Swiss slices and 2 pickle slices. Spread mustard generously on top halves; close sandwiches mustard-side down.

Brush skillet with 2 tbsp butter. Add 2 sandwiches bottom-side down; brush tops with 2 tbsp butter. Weight with a heavy skillet, pressing slightly. Toast, turning halfway, until bread is golden and cheese is melted, 6-8 minutes. Repeat with remaining sandwiches and butter.

---

Can substitute pulled pork for roasted pork. Based on a recipe by Jaelyn Luong, Allrecipes.
