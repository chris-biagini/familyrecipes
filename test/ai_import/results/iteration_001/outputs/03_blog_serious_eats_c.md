# Beef Wellington

Impressive but achievable.

Serves: 6
Category: Mains
Tags: holiday

## Sear and season beef.

- Beef tenderloin (center-cut), 2 lbs: Trimmed of silverskin and fat.
- Salt
- Black pepper
- Vegetable oil, 2 tbsp
- Horseradish (prepared), 2 tbsp
- Mustard (Dijon), 1 tbsp

Tie tenderloin at 1-inch intervals with butcher's twine. Season liberally with salt and pepper. Heat oil in a cast iron or stainless skillet over high until smoking. Sear without moving until well-browned on first side, about 2 minutes; rotate and continue until browned all over, about 10 minutes total. Transfer to a plate to cool.

Mix horseradish, mustard, and 1 tsp black pepper. Remove twine; rub tenderloin all over with the mixture. Refrigerate uncovered.

## Make duxelles; sear foie gras.

- Mushrooms, 1 lb: Roughly chopped.
- Butter (unsalted), 4 tbsp
- Shallots, 2 medium: Finely diced.
- Thyme, 2 tsp: Minced.
- Cognac, 1/2 cup
- Heavy cream, 1/2 cup
- Soy sauce, 2 tsp
- Foie gras (fresh), 4 oz
- Vegetable oil, 1/2 tsp

Pulse mushrooms in food processor in two batches until very finely chopped, about 10 pulses per batch.

Heat butter in a 12-inch skillet over medium-high. Add mushrooms and cook, stirring occasionally, until liquid is expelled and mushrooms begin sizzling, about 8 minutes. Continue until browned bits form on pan bottom, about 4 minutes more. Add shallots and thyme; cook until softened, about 2 minutes. Add Cognac, scrape up browned bits, and cook until nearly dry, about 4 minutes. Add cream and soy sauce; cook until thick and cohesive. Season with salt and pepper. Refrigerate.

Season foie gras with salt and pepper. Heat oil in a small cast iron skillet over high until smoking. Sear without moving, about 30 seconds per side. Transfer to paper towels. Pour rendered fat into mushroom mixture; stir to combine. Halve each piece of foie gras horizontally. Refrigerate everything at least 30 minutes.

## Assemble the roll.

- Phyllo dough, 1 sheet
- Prosciutto, 1/4 lb: Sliced paper thin.

Lay a double layer of plastic wrap (about 2 ft × 1 ft) on a cutting board. Lay phyllo on top. Shingle prosciutto over phyllo in a thin, overlapping layer, leaving a 2-inch border at the top and bottom edges. Spread mushroom mixture evenly over prosciutto.

Place tenderloin along the bottom edge. Top evenly with sliced foie gras. Using the plastic wrap to tighten as you go, roll tenderloin up in the ham/mushroom/phyllo. Rewrap tightly in fresh plastic wrap, twisting ends. Refrigerate at least 30 minutes.

## Wrap in puff pastry.

- Flour
- Puff pastry, 14 oz: Thawed.
- Egg, 1: Beaten.

Lightly flour board. Roll puff pastry into a rectangle at least 4 inches wider than the beef roll on the short side. Unwrap beef roll and place foie gras side up along the bottom edge of the pastry. Brush 6 inches of pastry above the roll with egg wash. Roll beef in pastry until completely wrapped, seam on the bottom; trim excess. Fold in the side flaps, fold down the top flap, trim the bottom flaps. Flip seam-side up and fold end flaps to seal; return right-side up. Transfer to a plate and refrigerate at least 30 minutes.

## Bake and serve.

- Sea salt (coarse)
- Chives, 1 bunch: Finely minced.

Preheat oven to 425°F. Transfer Wellington to a foil-lined baking sheet. Brush all over with remaining egg, score pastry decoratively, and sprinkle liberally with coarse salt. Bake until pastry is golden brown and center registers 110°F for rare or 120°F for medium-rare, 35-45 minutes. Rest 10 minutes.

Loosen from foil with a thin spatula and transfer to a carving board. Slice off ends, carve, and sprinkle cut surfaces with chives and more coarse salt.

---

A center-cut beef tenderloin is also known as Châteaubriand. Use high-quality all-butter puff pastry if possible (e.g., Dufour brand); homemade also works. Foie gras pâté can substitute for fresh foie gras — skip searing in Step 2 and instead spread pâté over the tenderloin during assembly in Step 3. Button, cremini, shiitake, portobello, or a mix all work for the mushrooms. Substitute any brandy or barrel-aged spirit (such as bourbon) for the Cognac. Spicy brown or hot English mustard can substitute for Dijon.
