# Caramelized Onion and Gruyère Biscuits

Big, cheesy, deeply savory.

Makes: 10 biscuits
Category: Baking
Tags: vegetarian, baked

## Caramelize onions.

- Butter (unsalted), 1 tbsp
- Olive oil, 1 tbsp
- Onions, 2 small: Halved and thinly sliced.

Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a baking sheet with parchment.

Melt butter with olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onions, reduce heat to low, cover and steam 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove lid; continue cooking until deep brown, 10-20 minutes more. Set aside to cool.

## Make dough; assemble.

- Flour (all-purpose), 2 3/4 cups (345 g)
- Sugar (white), 1 tbsp (15 g)
- Baking powder, 2 tsp
- Baking soda, 1 tsp
- Salt, 3/4 tsp
- Butter (unsalted), 8 tbsp: Cold, cut into 1/2-inch pieces.
- Gruyère, 4 oz (115 g): Cut into 1/2-inch cubes.
- Buttermilk, 1 cup

Combine flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Work cold butter into flour with fingertips or a pastry blender — or pulse in a food processor — until crumbly with no pieces larger than a pea. Stir in cheese.

Stir buttermilk into cooled onions, then add to flour mixture. Stir to combine — dough will seem dry; use hands to bring it together. A few floury spots are fine.

## Shape and bake.

- Flaky sea salt
- Black pepper

Turn dough onto a floured surface and roll to 1-inch thickness. Stamp out rounds with a floured 3-inch cutter; space on prepared sheet. Re-roll scraps as needed. Sprinkle with flaky salt and pepper.

Bake until deep golden-brown with bubbly cheese at the edges, 20-23 minutes. Eat warm.

---

Use coarse or kosher salt in the dough. Substitute another Swiss-style cheese for the gruyère. Make your own buttermilk if needed. Best on the first day; reheat on day two. Freeze unbaked rounds and thaw briefly before baking.

Based on a recipe from Alyce Shields at Pronto by Bar Bambino, via Tasting Table.
