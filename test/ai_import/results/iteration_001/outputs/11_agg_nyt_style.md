# No-Knead Bread

No effort, just time.

Makes: 1 loaf
Category: Bread
Tags: vegan, baked

## Mix and ferment.

- Flour (all-purpose), 3 1/3 cups (430 g): Plus more for dusting.
- Yeast (instant), generous 1/4 tsp (1 g)
- Salt (kosher), 2 tsp (8 g)
- Water, 1 1/2 cups (345 g)

Combine flour, yeast, and salt. Add water and stir until blended — dough will be shaggy and sticky. Cover and rest at least 12 hours, preferably 18, at about 70°F. Ready when surface is dotted with bubbles.

## Shape and proof.

- Cornmeal

Flour a work surface; turn out dough and fold over itself once or twice. Cover loosely and rest 15 minutes. Gently shape into a ball. Generously coat a cotton towel (not terry cloth) with flour or cornmeal; place dough seam side down and dust top with more. Cover with a second towel and let rise about 2 hours, until more than doubled and slow to spring back when poked.

## Bake.

At least 30 minutes before dough is ready, heat oven to 450°F with a 6-8 qt heavy covered pot inside. Carefully remove pot when ready. Turn dough in seam side up — it may look like a mess, that's OK. Shake pan once or twice if uneven. Cover and bake 30 minutes, then uncover and bake another 15-30 minutes until beautifully browned. Cool on rack.

---

Substitute wheat bran for cornmeal. Bread flour works equally well.

Based on a recipe from Jim Lahey via The New York Times.
