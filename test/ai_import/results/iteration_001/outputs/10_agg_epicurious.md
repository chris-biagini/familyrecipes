# Good Eats Beef Stew

Slow-braised short ribs, worth every hour.

Serves: 4
Category: Mains
Tags: comfort-food, american

## Braise short ribs.

- Tomato paste, 1/4 cup
- Apple cider vinegar, 1/4 cup
- Worcestershire sauce, 1 1/2 tsp
- Paprika, 1 tbsp
- Dried herbs, 1 1/2 tsp
- Short ribs (English-cut), 3 lbs
- Kosher salt, 1 tbsp

Whisk together tomato paste, vinegar, Worcestershire, paprika, and dried herbs in a large bowl.

Season short ribs with salt. Heat a large griddle over medium-high until a drop of water bounces off, then sear until browned on all sides. Transfer to the paste bowl and toss to coat. Wrap tightly in heavy-duty foil, place in a metal pan, and set in a cold oven on the middle rack. Set oven to 250°F and cook for 4 hours.

## Degrease braising liquid.

Remove foil package from oven. Carefully poke a hole and drain liquid into a heatproof container. Refrigerate until fat separates, about 1 hour, then transfer to the freezer until the fat cap solidifies, about 1 hour more. Let ribs rest at room temperature while liquid cools.

Remove solidified fat cap; measure out 1 tbsp and set aside. Reserve the remainder.

## Cook vegetables; finish and serve.

- Onion, 1 large: Thinly sliced.
- Kosher salt, 1 tsp
- Red potatoes, 1 lb: Unpeeled, sliced.
- Black pepper, a pinch
- Parsley, 1 tbsp: Chopped.

Melt the reserved tablespoon of fat in a large saucier over medium heat. Add onion and salt; cook 2-3 minutes, stirring to separate into rings. Add potatoes and black pepper, pour in the defatted braising liquid, and stir to combine. Cover tightly, reduce heat to low, and cook until potatoes are fork-tender, about 30 minutes.

Meanwhile, cut meat from bone, discarding connective tissue. Once potatoes are tender, uncover, nestle meat atop vegetables, cover, and cook 10 minutes more. Serve sprinkled with parsley.

---

Use any combination of thyme, oregano, and rosemary for the dried herbs. Reserve remaining fat for another use.
