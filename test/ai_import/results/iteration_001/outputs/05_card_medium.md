# Classic Beef Stew

A hearty, soul-warming beef stew with tender chunks of beef and root vegetables simmered low and slow.

Serves: 6
Category: Mains
Tags: comfort-food, one-pot, weeknight

## Brown the meat.

- Beef chuck roast, 2 lbs: Cut into 1 1/2-inch cubes.
- Flour (all-purpose), 3 tbsp
- Vegetable oil, 2 tbsp
- Salt
- Black pepper

Pat the beef dry with paper towels and toss with the flour, salt, and pepper until evenly coated. Heat the vegetable oil in a Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Working in batches, sear the beef on all sides until deeply browned, about 3-4 minutes per side. Transfer to a plate.

## Build the braise.

- Onion, 1 large: Diced.
- Garlic, 4 cloves: Minced.
- Dry red wine, 1 cup
- Beef broth, 2 cups
- Tomatoes (canned, diced), 1 can (14.5 oz)
- Tomato paste, 2 tbsp
- Bay leaves, 2
- Thyme (dried), 1 tsp

Add the diced onion to the pot and cook for 3-4 minutes until softened. Add the garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant. Pour in the red wine and scrape up the browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Let the wine reduce by about half, roughly 2-3 minutes.

Return the beef to the pot. Add the beef broth, diced tomatoes, tomato paste, bay leaves, and dried thyme. Stir to combine and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 1 hour.

## Add vegetables and finish.

- Potatoes (Yukon Gold), 3 medium: Cut into 1-inch cubes.
- Carrots, 3 large: Peeled and sliced into 1/2-inch rounds.
- Celery, 2 stalks: Sliced.

Add the potatoes, carrots, and celery. Cover and continue simmering for 45 minutes to 1 hour until the beef is fork-tender and the vegetables are cooked through. Remove the bay leaves. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. Serve in deep bowls with crusty bread.

---

Can be made in a slow cooker: brown the beef and sauté the onion and garlic first, then transfer everything to the slow cooker. Cook on low for 8-10 hours or high for 4-5 hours.
