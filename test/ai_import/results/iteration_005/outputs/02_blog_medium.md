# Easy Weeknight Chicken Stir-Fry

Serves: 4
Category: Mains
Tags: weeknight, quick

## Marinate chicken.

- Chicken thighs (boneless, skinless), 1 1/2 lbs: Cut into bite-sized pieces.
- Soy sauce, 2 tbsp
- Rice vinegar, 1 tbsp
- Cornstarch, 1 tsp

In a medium bowl, combine the chicken pieces with the soy sauce, rice vinegar, and cornstarch. Toss to coat evenly and let sit for at least 10 minutes while you prep the vegetables.

## Cook chicken.

- Vegetable oil, 1 tbsp

Heat the oil in a large wok or skillet over high heat until it just begins to smoke. Add the chicken in a single layer and cook without stirring for 2-3 minutes until golden on the bottom. Stir and cook another 2 minutes until cooked through. Transfer chicken to a plate.

## Cook vegetables.

- Vegetable oil, 1 tbsp
- Bell pepper (red), 1: Sliced into thin strips.
- Broccoli florets, 1 cup
- Carrots, 2 medium: Sliced on the bias.
- Green onions, 3: Cut into 1-inch pieces.
- Garlic, 3 cloves: Minced.
- Ginger (fresh), 1 tbsp: Minced.

Add the remaining oil to the wok. Add the bell pepper, broccoli, and carrots. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp. Add the green onions, garlic, and ginger and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.

## Finish with sauce.

- Soy sauce, 3 tbsp
- Sesame oil, 1 tbsp
- Honey, 1 tbsp
- Sriracha, 1 tsp

Whisk together the sauce ingredients in a small bowl. Return the chicken to the wok and pour the sauce over everything. Toss to coat and cook for 1-2 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly and everything is well coated.

Serve immediately over steamed rice.

---

You can substitute chicken breast for thighs, but be careful not to overcook them as they dry out faster than thighs. Adjust sriracha to taste.
