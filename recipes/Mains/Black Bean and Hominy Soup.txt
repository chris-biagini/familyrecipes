# Black Bean and Hominy Soup

Vaguely southwestern black bean soup.

## Soak beans.

- Black beans, 225 g: Check for debris.
- Water, 1000 g: Boiling.
- Salt, 5 g
- Black pepper
- Cumin

Combine all ingredients in pressure cooker. Cover and allow to soak for as much time as you have.

Refrigerate if soaking overnight.

## Pressure cook beans.

- Water

Once beans have soaked, add additional water, if necessary, to cover by one inch. Use Bean preset on pressure cooker and allow to release naturally.

## Saute aromatics and combine with beans.

- Olive oil, 75 g
- Onion, 1: Peel and coarsely chop.
- Bell pepper, 1: Peel and coarsely chop.
- Garlic, 4 cloves: Peel and coarsely chop.
- Tomato paste, 30 g
- Kombu, 1 piece: Optional.

In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onion and pepper. Cook until just barely golden. Add garlic and cook until fragrant. Add tomato paste and cook for another minute or so.

Add the cooked beans and their liquid to the pot. Add kombu. Bring to a simmer.

Taste beans and cook, if necessary, until just about done.

## Add rice and hominy.

- Rice, 100 g
- Hominy (canned), 15.5 ounces: Drain and rinse.
- Water

When beans are just about done, add the rice, hominy, and additional water to maintain consistency.

Continue cooking until rice is done, about 20 minutes.

## Finish and serve.

- Lime: Can sub vinegar.
- Salt
- Black pepper

Correct for acid and seasoning. Serve.
